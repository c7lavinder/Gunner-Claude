# Kyle Barks — Acquisition Manager

**Role:** Acquisition Manager (Closer)
**Team:** New Again Houses (NAH)
**Direct Reports:** Daniel Lozano (Lead Manager), Chris Segura (Lead Manager)

---

## Overview

Kyle Barks is the closer. As the sole Acquisition Manager at NAH, every qualified lead that Daniel and Chris generate ultimately lands on Kyle's desk. His job is to convert qualified appointments into signed contracts — and his data shows he can do it. Kyle has the highest concentration of A-grade calls on the team (70 out of 288 graded), and his best work demonstrates mastery of the full 10-Step Close: rapport building, motivation extraction, benefit framing, offer delivery, and commitment securing. His challenge is consistency — maintaining that A-level execution across all call types, especially when facing objections or stalled leads.

## What Kyle Does Well

**Rapport Building with Distressed Sellers.** Kyle's strongest skill is his ability to connect with sellers who are going through difficult life circumstances. His coaching feedback consistently highlights his empathy with grieving widows, divorcing couples, and financially stressed homeowners. In one standout call with a seller named Anita — who had recently lost both her husband and son-in-law — Kyle demonstrated genuine compassion while still advancing the conversation toward a deal: "So just, and I'm very sorry to hear that, Anita. So just recently, your son-in-law passed away, and your husband passed away?" He then connected her emotional pain to the practical benefit of selling: "We can kind of put this all behind you and get you paid that check, which seems like it's the most important thing."

**Confident Offer Delivery.** When Kyle delivers an offer, he does it with conviction and strategic silence. His coaching notes highlight his technique of stating the number and then pausing to let the seller respond: "With all that being said, I have approval from my underwriting and construction to purchase the property at $140,000." The pause that follows is deliberate — it forces the seller to react rather than allowing Kyle to negotiate against himself. This is a direct application of Todd Toback's "Delivering the Offer" technique.

**Operational Follow-Through.** Kyle excels at the administrative side of closing. He guides sellers through electronic document signing with patience and clarity, confirms lockbox codes and utility status, schedules construction walkthroughs, and ensures every operational detail is handled. His coaching feedback notes: "Kyle was highly effective in guiding the seller through the electronic signature process, providing clear, step-by-step instructions." This attention to post-contract execution is what separates closers who get contracts signed from closers who get deals closed.

**Flexibility Across Call Types.** Kyle handles a wide range of call types — offer calls, admin callbacks, contract signings, walkthrough scheduling, and follow-ups. His 103 admin calls (the highest on the team) show he is deeply involved in the operational pipeline, not just the closing moment. This breadth of involvement gives him context that makes his offer calls more informed.

**Reading Emotional Situations.** Kyle has a strong instinct for reading the emotional temperature of a call and adjusting his approach accordingly. With distressed sellers, he slows down and leads with empathy. With business-minded sellers, he shifts to a more direct, numbers-focused approach. This adaptability maps directly to Steve Trang's Personality-Modified Approach to Selling (PMAS).

## Where Kyle Needs Development

**Losing Control to Talkative Sellers.** Kyle's most consistent weakness across his lower-scoring calls is allowing sellers to take control of the conversation. When a seller launches into a long story, presents a competing offer, or redirects the conversation away from the offer, Kyle sometimes follows rather than leads. His coaching feedback notes: "Kyle needs to immediately take control of the call by following the intro and stage-setting script." Implementing Todd's Reversing technique — responding to a seller's statement with a question that redirects back to the agenda — would address this directly.

**Defensive Responses to Objections.** In a few documented calls, Kyle became defensive when facing difficult objections, particularly around title issues and competing offers. One coaching note flagged phrases like "I don't want to be the bad guy" and "I can't do everyone's job for them" as defensive language that undermines the seller's confidence. The coaching principle is to acknowledge the objection with empathy first, then redirect: "I understand this is frustrating. Let me see what we can do to help."

**Stage-Setting on Offer Calls.** Kyle's lower-scoring calls frequently skip the opening stage-setting step. The coaching feedback consistently recommends starting every offer call with a clear framework: "I'll review what we discussed, share what our team found, and present our offer. If it works, great. If not, no hard feelings. Sound fair?" This small addition creates psychological safety and gives Kyle permission to control the conversation.

**Re-Qualifying Stalled Leads.** When leads go cold or circumstances change, Kyle sometimes treats the follow-up call as a casual check-in rather than a re-qualification opportunity. His coaching notes suggest that every follow-up should include a motivation check: "Last time we spoke, you mentioned [motivation]. Is that still the case?" This ensures Kyle is not investing time in leads whose motivation has evaporated.

**Price Anchoring Before the Offer.** While Kyle delivers offers confidently, he does not always anchor the seller's expectations before presenting the number. Using the price anchoring technique — "Other investors in your area are buying properties at 40–50% of comparable value. That's not our offer, just the market context" — would soften the impact of the offer and reduce sticker shock.

## Communication Style

Kyle's natural communication style is **empathetic, patient, and relationship-focused**. He builds trust through genuine interest in the seller's situation and demonstrates emotional intelligence that is rare in sales. He is at his best in longer conversations where he has time to develop rapport, extract deep motivation, and frame the offer as a solution to the seller's specific problem.

His style maps most closely to the **Amiable** personality type with strong **Expressive** tendencies — he leads with empathy, values relationships, and is comfortable with emotional conversations. This is a significant strength when dealing with distressed sellers, but he should practice being more assertive and structured when dealing with Driver-type sellers who want efficiency and directness.

Kyle's empathy is his superpower. The coaching focus should be on adding structure and assertiveness around that empathy — not replacing it — so that he can maintain control of conversations while still making sellers feel heard.

## Objection Response Patterns

Based on Kyle's graded calls, the following objection types appear most frequently, along with recommended responses:

| Objection | Frequency | Recommended Response |
|-----------|-----------|---------------------|
| "I have another offer / my friend wants to buy it" | High | "That's great that you have options. Help me understand — what would need to be true for our offer to make more sense than theirs?" |
| "Your offer is too low" | High | "I hear you. What number were you hoping I was going to say?" (Then listen and work from there) |
| "I need to talk to my [family member]" | Medium | "Absolutely. When you talk to them, what do you think their biggest concern will be? Let's address that now so you can present them with the full picture." |
| "I'm not in a rush" | Medium | "That makes sense. If we could agree on a number that works, would there be any reason not to move forward now rather than later?" |
| "The title has issues" | Low | "We deal with title issues regularly. Let me get our title team involved — they can usually sort this out. What specifically is the situation?" |
