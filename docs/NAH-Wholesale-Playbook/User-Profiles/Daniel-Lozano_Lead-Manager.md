# Daniel Lozano — Lead Manager

**Role:** Lead Manager
**Team:** New Again Houses (NAH)
**Reports To:** Kyle Barks (Acquisition Manager)

## Overview

Daniel Lozano is the high-volume engine of the NAH lead management operation. With 3,800 total calls logged — the highest on the team — Daniel embodies the volume mindset that drives wholesale pipeline generation. His primary function is qualifying inbound and outbound leads, scheduling appointments for the Acquisition Manager, and ensuring no lead falls through the cracks. Daniel's work ethic and call volume are his defining characteristics, and his organizational discipline around scheduling and logistics is a consistent strength.

## What Daniel Does Well

**Volume and Consistency.** Daniel's 3,800 calls represent a relentless commitment to the phone. He understands that wholesaling is a numbers game and executes accordingly. His outbound-heavy ratio (98.5% outbound) shows he is proactively working the pipeline rather than waiting for leads to come to him.

**Scheduling and Logistics.** Daniel's highest-scoring calls consistently involve appointment scheduling, rescheduling, and coordination. He excels at confirming specific times, clarifying time zones, setting text reminders, and ensuring the seller knows exactly who will call and when. His top-graded calls show phrases like "I'll schedule it for tomorrow at 10 a.m." and "You're going to receive a text message just as a confirmation for the call." This operational precision is critical for pipeline velocity.

**Clear, Direct Communication.** Daniel gets to the point. His call openings are efficient and professional, typically identifying himself, his company, and the property address within the first 15 seconds. He does not waste the seller's time with unnecessary preamble, which aligns well with the Driver personality type in Steve Trang's PMAS framework.

**Professional Tone.** Across his graded calls, Daniel maintains a consistently professional and courteous demeanor. Even when sellers are uncooperative or conversations go sideways, he remains composed. His coaching feedback frequently notes his "professional and courteous tone" as a strength.

**Next-Step Discipline.** Daniel rarely ends a call without a defined next action. Whether it is a callback time, a walkthrough appointment, or a partner introduction, he closes conversations with specificity — a direct application of Todd Toback's "Getting Commitments" technique.

---

## Where Daniel Needs Development

**Letting Sellers Dominate the Conversation.** In his lower-scoring calls, a recurring pattern emerges: talkative sellers take control of the conversation, and Daniel struggles to redirect. One coaching note observed that "the seller dominated the conversation, preventing Daniel from following the script." Developing polite interruption techniques and using Todd's Reversing method would help him regain control without alienating the seller.

**Motivation Probing Depth.** While Daniel is efficient at gathering logistics (property condition, timeline, price expectations), he sometimes skips the deeper "why" behind the seller's decision. His coaching feedback repeatedly suggests asking follow-up questions like "What even had you thinking about selling?" or "What happens if you don't sell?" — the core qualification questions that separate a warm lead from a hot one.

**Leading with Price.** In some lower-scoring calls, Daniel references previous offers or price points too early in the conversation, which puts the seller on the defensive before motivation has been established. The coaching principle here is clear: diagnose before prescribing. Price should come after motivation, timeline, and condition are fully understood.

**Setting Call Expectations.** Daniel's top calls jump straight into the conversation, but his coaching feedback consistently recommends adding a brief expectation-setting statement at the start: "Is it okay if I share how we work? I'll ask questions about your property and situation. If not a fit, let's say so. If yes, I'll schedule with our franchise owner to present an offer. Fair?" This small addition reduces seller resistance and creates a framework for the rest of the call.

**Accepting Vague Timelines.** When sellers give non-specific responses like "maybe next week" or "sometime soon," Daniel sometimes accepts these without pinning down a specific date and time. Converting vague commitments into concrete appointments is a skill that would directly increase his conversion rate.

## Communication Style

Daniel's natural communication style is **efficient and organized**. He is task-oriented, prefers structure, and excels in situations where the path forward is clear. He is at his best when coordinating logistics between sellers and the AM, and when working through a high volume of calls with consistent process.

His style maps most closely to the **Driver** personality type — direct, results-focused, and time-conscious. This is a strength when dealing with sellers who are also Drivers, but he should practice adapting his pace and emotional engagement when dealing with Expressive or Amiable sellers who need more warmth and reassurance before they open up.
