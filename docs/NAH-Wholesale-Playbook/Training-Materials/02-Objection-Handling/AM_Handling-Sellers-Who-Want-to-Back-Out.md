# Handling Sellers Who Want to Back Out

**Stage:** Post-appointment — seller is under contract or verbally committed

## Why This Matters
Sellers backing out kills deals that are already in your pipeline. Every deal saved here is pure profit — no new lead cost, no new qualification time. This is the highest-ROI AM skill.

## The 4 Common Reasons Sellers Back Out

### 1. "I changed my mind / I'm not sure anymore"
**What's really happening:** Fear. They're scared of making a big decision.

**Talk Track:**
"I totally understand — this is a big decision, and it's normal to have second thoughts. Can I ask what specifically changed? Because when we first talked, you mentioned [original motivation]. Has that situation changed, or is it more that you're just feeling nervous about it?"

**Key moves:**
- Revisit their original pain point — remind them WHY they reached out
- Don't pressure. Empathize first, then ask questions
- "What would need to be true for you to feel comfortable moving forward?"
- If they truly don't want to sell, respect it — but plant the seed: "No problem at all. If anything changes, you have my number."

### 2. "My family / spouse / kids say I shouldn't sell"
**What's really happening:** Outside influence overriding their decision.

**Talk Track:**
"I hear that a lot — family always has opinions, especially about the house. Would it help if I talked with [family member] directly? Sometimes they just have questions I can answer, and it takes the pressure off you."

**Key moves:**
- Offer to include the family member in the conversation
- Ask: "What are their specific concerns?" — then address each one
- Sometimes the family member doesn't understand the situation (repairs needed, financial pressure, etc.)
- Never talk badly about the family's input — validate it, then educate
- "I totally get it. [Spouse] wants to make sure you're getting a fair deal. Let me walk through the numbers with both of you."

### 3. "I got another offer / I want to list with an agent"
**What's really happening:** They think they can get more money.

**Talk Track:**
"That's great that you're exploring your options — you should. Can I ask what the other offer looks like? Because sometimes what looks like a higher number actually nets you less after commissions, repairs, closing costs, and the timeline."

**Key moves:**
- Don't trash the competition. Stay professional.
- Break down the NET comparison:
  - Agent listing: sale price minus 6% commission, minus repairs/staging, minus 3-6 months of carrying costs (mortgage, taxes, insurance, utilities)
  - Other wholesaler: what's their actual close timeline? Are they even funded?
  - Your offer: cash, as-is, close on their timeline, no fees
- "I'm not trying to talk you out of anything. I just want to make sure you're comparing apples to apples."
- If they have a legit better offer, congratulate them and move on gracefully — they'll remember you

### 4. "The price is too low / I want more money"
**What's really happening:** Price objection disguised as backing out.

**Talk Track:**
"I hear you. Help me understand — what number would make you feel good about this? Because I want to see if there's a way we can make this work for both of us."

**Key moves:**
- Get their number first — don't negotiate against yourself
- If their number is unrealistic, educate with data: "Based on the condition and the repairs needed, here's what the property would sell for fixed up... here's what repairs cost... here's what we can offer and still make this work."
- Sometimes you can adjust terms instead of price: faster close, flexible move-out date, cover moving costs
- If the gap is too big: "I understand. I can't get to that number and still make the deal work. But if things change or if the property sits on the market, call me back — I'll still be buying."

## Universal Principles

### DO:
- Listen more than you talk. The reason they're backing out is in what they say.
- Revisit the original motivation. "When we first spoke, you said [X]. Has that changed?"
- Stay calm and professional. Desperation kills deals.
- Give them an out. Paradoxically, removing pressure often keeps them in.
- Follow up. Many sellers who "back out" come back in 2-4 weeks. Set a task.
- Document the reason. Add a note in GHL — this data helps the whole team.

### DON'T:
- Don't guilt trip or pressure
- Don't get emotional or take it personally
- Don't trash other offers or agents
- Don't make promises you can't keep to save the deal
- Don't burn the bridge — today's "no" is next month's "yes"

## When to Escalate to Corey
- Seller is under contract and threatening legal action
- Seller's attorney has reached out
- Deal is $30K+ assignment fee and price renegotiation might save it
- Seller is making claims about misrepresentation
