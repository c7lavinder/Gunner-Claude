# 🛡️ Common Brush-Offs & How to Handle Them

> Brush-offs are **reflexes, not objections.** The seller isn't saying "no" to your offer — they're saying "no" to being on a sales call. Your job is to get past the reflex and into a real conversation.

**The rule:** One calm redirect. If they hold firm, respect it and move on.

---

## 🗣️ The Brush-Offs

### 1. "I'm not interested."

> **You:** "Totally understand — most people say that at first. I'm not trying to sell you anything. We just buy houses as-is in your area. Is the property on [Address] something you'd ever consider selling, even down the road?"

---

### 2. "Take me off your list."

> **You:** "Absolutely, I'll take care of that right now. Before I do — just so I mark it correctly — is this a property you're planning to keep long-term, or is it more of a timing thing?"

⚠️ **If they insist:** "Done. You're off the list. Have a great day." — Then **actually remove them.** See the DNC Compliance guide.

---

### 3. "I already have an agent."

> **You:** "Oh nice, how's that going for you? … Has it been on the market long? … The reason I ask is we buy as-is — no showings, no repairs, no commissions. Sometimes that's a better fit depending on the situation."

---

### 4. "How did you get my number?"

> **You:** "Your property showed up in our system as a potential match for what we're looking for in the Nashville area. We reach out to homeowners directly — no pressure, just seeing if there's any interest."

---

### 5. "I'm not selling."

> **You:** "No problem at all. A lot of the people we talk to aren't actively selling — they're just open to hearing a number. If I could get you a cash offer with no obligation, would that at least be worth a quick conversation?"

---

### 6. "What's your offer?"

> **You:** "Great question — I can't give you an exact number right now because we'd need to know a little more about the property. That's actually why I'm calling. Can I ask you a couple quick questions so we can see if it makes sense?"

---

### 7. "This is a scam."

> **You:** "I totally get why you'd be cautious — there's a lot of junk calls out there. We're a real local company here in Nashville called New Again Houses. You can Google us right now if you want. We've been buying homes in the area for years."

---

### 8. "I don't have time right now."

> **You:** "No worries at all — when's a better time to catch you for just a couple minutes? I can call you back whenever works."

💡 **Key:** Get a **specific** callback time. Not "later" — a day and time.

---

### 9. "The house isn't for sale."

> **You:** "I hear you. We're not looking for houses that are listed — we actually prefer to work directly with homeowners. If the right offer came along, is that something you'd ever consider?"

---

### 10. "I need to talk to my spouse/family."

> **You:** "Of course — that makes total sense. What if I get some basic info from you now, and then you can chat with them? I can call back [tomorrow/day after] to see where you're at. What time works?"

---

### 11. "You guys lowball people."

> **You:** "I appreciate you being upfront. We're not trying to rip anyone off — we buy houses as-is, which means we take on all the repairs, closing costs, and risk. Our numbers reflect that, but we're always transparent about how we get there. Fair enough?"

---

### 12. "I want full market value."

> **You:** "Totally understand. And some houses, listing on the market is the best move. We're a better fit for people who want speed, convenience, and no hassle — sell as-is, close in a couple weeks, no showings. If that's ever appealing, we're here."

---

### 13. "Send me something in the mail/email."

> **You:** "I can definitely do that. What's the best email to send it to? … And just so I send you the right info — is there anything specific about the property situation I should know about?"

💡 This is often a polite brush-off. Get the email, but **also try to keep the conversation going.**

---

### 14. "I already got an offer from someone."

> **You:** "Oh nice — are you pretty happy with it? … Sometimes it's worth getting a second look just to make sure you're getting the best deal. We could have one of our team members take a quick look and give you a comparison — no obligation."

---

### 15. "Stop calling me."

> **You:** "I apologize for the inconvenience. I'm removing your number right now. You won't hear from us again. Have a good day."

🚨 **Hard stop.** Remove immediately. Log it. No second attempt.

---

### 16. "How much are you going to offer?"

> **You:** "It depends on the property — condition, location, repairs needed. I don't want to throw out a random number that doesn't mean anything. Can I ask a few quick questions so we can give you something real?"

---

### 17. "I'm renting it out / I have tenants."

> **You:** "Got it — is that going well for you? … Some landlords get to a point where they're tired of dealing with tenants and maintenance. If you ever get there, we buy rental properties too — even with tenants in place."

---

### 18. "Call me back in [vague timeframe]."

> **You:** "Happy to do that. Let me put it on my calendar — would [specific date] around [time] work? That way I'm not catching you at a bad time again."

💡 **Always pin it down.** "Call me in a few months" becomes "How about the first week of May?"

---

## 🎯 The Golden Rules of Brush-Offs

| Rule | Why |
|---|---|
| **Never argue** | Agree first, then redirect |
| **One redirect attempt** | If they hold firm, respect it |
| **"Take me off your list" = do it immediately** | No exceptions |
| **Stay calm and friendly** | Your tone matters more than your words |
| **Every brush-off is intel** | "I have an agent" = they've thought about selling. Log everything. |

---

## 📋 Quick Reference

1. **Brush-offs are reflexes, not real objections** — one calm redirect can open the conversation
2. **Agree first, then pivot** — "Totally understand" before anything else
3. **"Take me off the list" and "Stop calling" = immediate compliance** — no exceptions
4. **Always try to pin down a callback time** — "Call me later" isn't a time
5. **Log everything** — even a brush-off gives you information for next time
