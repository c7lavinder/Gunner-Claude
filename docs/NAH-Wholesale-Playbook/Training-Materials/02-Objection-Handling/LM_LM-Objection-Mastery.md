# LM Objection Mastery

**Role:** Lead Manager
**Category:** Objection Handling

> Best Practices for Handling Objections

---

Lead Manager Onboarding --> Objection Mastery    

1. What are objections? 
Objections are a seller’s response to you when they are
Trying to push you away
Give you an excuse they come up with when they are afraid to tell you no
It could just be a part of their problem that we have to help solve.
2. What are the basic objections you will run across and how do you overcome them? 
The 3 categories that most objections will fall under are Price, Timeline, and Other People Involved.
For this reason, we designed these rebuttals to the objections because they work well. So, it is important that you follow the process.
Price objections  → The seller’s disagreement on price or their refusal to give us their price. **the goal of this rebuttal is to just find out the seller’s best price**
Example 1: The seller saying our anchor offer is too low
Overcoming: “Okay I understand, again that is not our offer it’s just what I see other cash investors buying similar properties for. What were you hoping I was going to at least say?” →Example 1 Recording ←
Example 2: The seller refusing to give us a price they were looking for (You called me first)
Overcoming: “If we were to buy it completely as-is, cover all closing cost, and close on your timeline, do you at least have a 10k - 15k range you were hoping for” →Example 2 Recording ←
Timeline objections → The seller needs more time to close than the traditional 30 days for reasons x, y, or z. **This is where we are able to be flexible and operate on the seller’s timeline, but we do want to get it done as soon as we can.**
Example 1: The seller needs at least 60-90 days because they have a lot of stuff and don’t want to be rushed on moving out.
Overcoming: “I understand and we don’t want you to feel rushed, if we were able to hire some movers to go out there and help you would you feel more comfortable with closing sooner?”→Example 1 Recording ←
Example 2: The seller isn’t the owner but their mom died and they need to get rid of it
Overcoming: “We can go ahead and get the probate process started for you if we can get an agreement in place, but we will not be able to close on it until that is complete.” →Example 2 Recording ←
Example 3: The seller has to give the tenant 60 days notice
Overcoming: “Okay that is perfectly fine. We can go ahead and get an agreement in place and write a 60 day closing.”→Example 3 Recording ←
Example 4: Taxes, the seller will have to pay too much in taxes if they sell the property this year. 
Overcoming: “Okay I completely understand that, nobody wants to pay more taxes than they already have to. Would you be comfortable getting an agreement in place and closing on the property next year?” → Example 4 Recording ←
Other People Involved → This affects the ability for them to make the choice of who they want to sell the property to, or even just if they will sell the property. *That is why we set the expectations that all decision makers must be present when we present you with our number.*
Example 1: Partner that is on the deed but not available 
Overcoming: “When do you think would be a good time where the two of you would be together for my home buying specialist to give you both a call? We can even do a 3 way call if that is easier.” → Example 1 Recording ← 
3. How do you overcome an objection? 
Overcoming an objection is your way of answering, going around, or solving the problem/objection that the seller gave to you. 
If they say that your anchor offer is too low then you respond with “What were you hoping I would say?”. 
If they don’t give you a price when you ask then you say “Okay but if we can get you an as-is cash offer, cover all closing costs, and close on your timeline do you at least have a 10k-15k range you were hoping for?”
If they say they need more time so they’re not rushed when they’re moving their stuff out you say, “okay no worries we can close when you’re ready, but it probably wouldn’t make things easier on you if we got a couple of guys out there to help you move?”
If they tell you that they need to talk it over with their wife then you say, “okay perfect, when will both of you be available so I can schedule the call with my home buying specialist?”
We want to get it done as fast as we can so if we need to get movers out there then that is what we will do, just don’t over promise anything.  We always want to make sure that all decision makers are present during the appointment so that we can ensure the Acquisition Manager will get a confident yes or no from the seller.
4. How did I master objections? 
The best way to master objections is by doing. 
That includes 
Practice by doing role plays
Writing them down and studying
Having them thrown at you in a live call and just doing it
 When I first started I wrote down every objection I knew in my notebook and then the appropriate response under each of them, that way when I was on the call or in a role play, if the seller threw an objection at me then I could look down into my notebook and pick out what objection it was and what I need to respond with. Just by doing that on every single call and roleplaying every single day I was able to just have it become second nature. The good thing is now that we have developed the script, a lot of the objections are taken care of by setting the expectations. 
5. What is a 3rd party story, and why do we use them? 
 3rd party story is our way of establishing two very important things with sellers 
More credibility
Trust  
The seller tells us their problem or objection and we give them a story in the past of how we have helped a seller before with the same situation. It shows them that we are qualified to fix whatever it is they need because we have done it before in the past. 
Sometimes it can be a true problem the seller has that we can help solve 
Sometimes it is the seller throwing out an objection at you and this is a good way to overcome it 
	

6. How to execute a 3rd party story. 
The way we use 3rd party stories is literally taking the exact thing the seller says and telling them we have had seller’s in the past with the same situation that we were able to help out. 
Timeline 
Seller-  “I have too much stuff I would need to move out, I would need at least 60 days.”
LM-  “Okay I completely understand and Mr. Seller we have had many sellers in the past that are in your exact situation. We were able to help them with moving and they were able to get it all done in 30 days, do you think that would be something that would be of benefit to you?”  →  Timeline 3rd party story Recording  ←
