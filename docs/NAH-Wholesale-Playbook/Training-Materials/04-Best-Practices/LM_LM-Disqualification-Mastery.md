# LM Disqualification Mastery

**Role:** Lead Manager
**Category:** Best Practices

> How to disqualify a non motivated seller quickly and not waste time. 

---

Lead Manager Onboarding --> Disqualification Mastery

What does it mean to Disqualify and why do we do it?
We want to disqualify sellers as fast as we can, usually under 4 minutes.
What?- Disqualifying a seller is trying to get a seller that does not meet our standards to become an appointment off the phone so that we can move onto the next one. As a Lead Manager, your job is to set appointments for the Acquisition Manager as efficiently as possible. When you disqualify, you are listening to all of the red flags that sellers can throw up that would indicate to you that they are not a good fit for us. 
Why?- We need to disqualify in under 4 minutes so you can not waste time on leads that aren’t a fit for us. We can move onto the next lead and try to either set them up for an appointment or disqualify them too. 
What keywords do we look for and how do we disqualify? 
The most important thing we pay attention to when trying to set up an appointment is motivation, so we’re listening for indicators that show us if their motivation is true or not.
Examples of things we would hear that would tell us to start disqualifying would be things like- 
 “you called me first”, “I’m just hearing offers”, “this is an unsolicited call”, “I’m not looking to sell but I will for the right price”. 
Another key indicator that we use to disqualify is if the seller doesn’t want to cooperate with setting the expectations. 
Responses are short and they don’t want to talk to us,they just want us to give them our number. Once you have heard those indicators then you should immediately go straight into motivation and determine if they will be a good fit or not.



Identifying a RED seller
A RED seller is someone who is hostile, rude, or irritable. 
 It sounds like they don’t want to talk to you. This could be because they get a lot of calls every day, maybe it’s their personality, or maybe they are just having a bad day. Regardless we have to maintain our professional bearing and not go red back against them. We need to decide with RED sellers at what point do we just disqualify and get off the call and when do we keep going to try and get them to open up.  
Ex: Red Seller- “I’ve been getting offers from multiple companies and I’m just looking around for other offers”   
 LM- “Okay so you’re just looking around for the best offer, what are some of those offers you received?”    
Seller- “that is none of your business!”  
Trying to get the seller un RED- Once you have identified the RED seller and decided you should keep moving forward you need to try to get their guard down a little bit. 
An excellent way of doing this is by building rapport. If you can establish some kind of trust or find some common ground with the red seller you might have a chance of getting their guard down. There is a good possibility that it is just their personality, but you will be able to read that. The only reason to keep moving forward with a RED seller is if you sense some motivation, otherwise just push away and disqualify.
