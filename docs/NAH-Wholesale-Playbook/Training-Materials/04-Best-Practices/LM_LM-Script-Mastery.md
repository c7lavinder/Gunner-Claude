# LM Script Mastery

**Role:** Lead Manager
**Category:** Best Practices

> Best Practices to Master LM Script

---

Lead Manager Onboarding --> Script Mastery
1. What is the goal of the entire script- 
We use this script to not only qualify, but disqualify leads as fast as we can.  
We want to talk to motivated sellers so that  
We can set the Acquisition Managers aka (Home Buying Specialist) up for an appointment with the goal of getting the seller under contract while we want to get unmotivated sellers off the phone as quickly and efficiently as we can. 
2. What is this goal of each part of the script, and why are they important?  
Intro- The first part is the introduction. 
We want to have a simple introduction stating who we are and why we are calling then state 
“I probably caught you at a bad time”
The importance of this is to verify we are talking to the right person 
They know who we are and
 to ensure it is an appropriate time for a 5-10 minute conversation

Setting Expectations- The second part is setting the expectations of this call.
 We want to let the sellers know from the very beginning 
what this call is going to look like
what it would like to work with us if they did decide they wanted to move forward  and
 establish a rule of being comfortable letting each other know if either party thinks we will not be able to work together
The importance of setting expectations for the call is so that the sellers know exactly what we will be doing on this call. This will ensure the call will go as smoothly as possible and the sellers will have no questions about how we work. 
Confirm Property Condition- The next part is confirming the property condition.
 If it is already there from previous notes or text messages then confirm if those are correct or any changes have been made
 If it is not, then you will need to gather the property’s condition by going through the list that is on salesforce starting with 
number of beds and baths
 ending with all or anything else that isn’t listed already
 It is important to know this for us to determine how much work the property needs and how much we can buy it or sell it for. 
Confirm Roadblocks- Following that is confirming the road blocks.
 A road block is simply anything that would slow us down from buying the property.
Example 1: Other decision makers
Example 2: Tenants
Example 3: Timeline- “If we could agree on a price today usually we can close within 30 days, would that be okay with you or do you need some more time?”
Example 4: Anything other than price that would stop us from working together
It is important to make sure we have uncovered all of the roadblocks so that we can make sure nothing is in our way of beginning to get an agreement in place with the seller ASAP. 
MOTIVATION- Next and probably the most important is motivation.
 We need to figure out why the seller wants to sell. 
What problem do they have that we can help solve because that is what we are, problem solvers. 
Digging into the motivation of the seller can be easy or it can be very difficult depending on the type of seller we are dealing with. 
The more rapport you have with the seller, the easier it will be. 
The more pain we can pull out of the seller, the deeper discount we will be able to get on the property. 99% of the time, sellers will act based upon emotion which is why we want to pull out their pain before going into price. 
We have a list of questions to be asked that will help extract pain. 
How long has that been going on? 
Can you tell me more about that? 
What have you tried to do about that? 
A lot of times asking these questions can be uncomfortable, but it is very important to follow through so we can pull out all of the pain. 
Price- Right after motivation comes price. 
We never ever want to be the first ones to give out a price. 
If we show them our hand first, then they will just take our number and shop it around. 
It is important for us to see the number they are hoping to get to help determine if we will be a good fit for them or not 
The first number they give us is NOT going to be their best, so we try and dig a little bit more into what actually is their best number before we present it to the “Home Buying Specialist '' or Acquisition Managers.
During this part of the script we will also give them a price anchor 
50% - 60% of the Zillow Zestimate 
This is a way of trying to find what their actual best price is to see if we will be able to work together or not. 
Setting Expectations For the Next Call- This is the end of the script. 
It is broken up into two parts 
If we are NOT a good fit 
If we are a good fit
If you determine at any point during the call that we will NOT be a good fit 
Then you will go straight to this portion
You will tell them that you have everything you need and you do not believe they will be a good fit to work with our company
You will push away by telling them you think their best option would be to go with someone else or with a realtor
 You will let them know you will follow up with them in the future to see if there are any changes
FOLLOW UP is key 
If you determine that they are a good fit 
You will be setting them up for an appointment for either a property walkthrough or offer call with the acquisition manager. If we can walk the house in the next 48 hours set up a walkthrough for ASAP (Same day if possible). For walkthroughs set the expectation we will come walk and then give you an offer later that day. For offer calls You need to be setting the expectations for the next call with the “Home Buying Specialist” that will present them with the number their property is qualified for. 
Rapport- Rapport is an established trust between two people. This does not have a specific part in the script because it can be at any point of the call. Building rapport is finding common ground and having some kind of bond with the seller. It is very important that the seller trusts you and trusts us to help them solve their problem or else we probably will never get the deal.


3. What is tonality, and why is it important? 
We want to be able to match the tone and the speed of the seller. 
If the seller is talking at a high fast paced pitch, then you want to try your best to match that and vice versa.
No matter how high, low, fast, or slow the seller is talking, you need to be able to match that. 
It is important to do that because 
In the seller’s mind, if we are talking too fast then we are too much like a “used car salesman” and that will deter them from working with us. 
If we are too slow then they might think we aren’t intelligent enough to solve their problem. 
When we are talking about motivation 
We need to be at a low, soft tone to show that we are empathetic to their problem and want to be able to help 
Overall the importance of tonality helps establish rapport throughout the conversation. 
4. What is a price anchor, and why do we do it?  
A price anchor is a $20k range that we tell the sellers we see similar properties to theirs being bought for in cash in their area. 
We do this to
Reset the seller’s expectations of what their property could be worth 
To get a lower price from the seller so we can buy it at a deeper discount 
The price anchor or “investor range” is 50% - 60% of the zillow zestimate
5. When do we price anchor (no price, high price, discounted price) 
*We price anchor NO MATTER WHAT!*
Every call that you make to the price portion of the script, you will always price anchor 
If they refuse to give you a price, then you will price anchor them
If the price they give you is really high, then you will price anchor 
If the price they give you is already discounted, then you will still give them a price anchor 
We always want to price anchor them so we can truly find what their best price is and get the property at more of a discount
6. How do we pitch it for each situation  
Example 1: Asking for the seller’s price
LM- “Did you have a price in mind you were looking to get?”
Seller-  “No I was hoping you would give me an offer”
LM- “If we were able to buy the property as-is, cover all closing costs, and close on your timeline do you at least have some sort of range you were looking to get?”
Seller-  “No, make me an offer” 
LM-  “I can have my Home Buying Specialist give you a call, but to better assist them do they have at least a 10k - 15k range off the record of what you were hoping for?” 
Seller-  “No, what is your number?!”
LM- *assume the zestimate is $200k* “Okay Mr. Seller, no problem. This isn’t our offer but I see that other investors are buying similar properties to yours for cash around $80k - $100k, what would you say if someone offered you that?”  
→Price Anchor Recording←
The same process will apply if they give you a high price or a low price
You will ask them if that is the best they can do given we are able to buy the property as-is, cover all closing costs, and close on their timeline and then move into the price anchor.  
If the seller likes the price anchor and says they would like to move forward with a number within that range
 then you will proceed to set them up for an appointment with the Home Buying Specialist 
If the seller says no it is way too low (the usual answer) 
You will ask them “what were you hoping I would at least say”. That is how you get their lower price.
