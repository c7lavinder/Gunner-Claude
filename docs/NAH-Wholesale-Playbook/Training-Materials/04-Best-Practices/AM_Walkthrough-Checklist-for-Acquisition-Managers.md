# 🏠 Walkthrough Checklist for Acquisition Managers

> You're there for two reasons: **assess the property** and **build rapport with the seller.** Both matter equally. A great assessment with no rapport = seller picks the other investor. Great rapport with a sloppy assessment = you overpay or miss something big.

---

## ✅ Before You Arrive

- [ ] Review lead notes in GHL — know their name, situation, motivation
- [ ] Check comps in the area — know the ARV ballpark
- [ ] Charge your phone (lots of photos)
- [ ] Bring tape measure, flashlight, notepad
- [ ] Confirm the appointment — call or text 30-60 min before

---

## 👋 Arrival & First Impression

- **Arrive on time** or 5 minutes early. Never late.
- **Park in the street,** not the driveway (unless invited)
- **Dress clean and professional** — polo or button-down, clean shoes
- **Greet warmly by name:** *"Hey [Name], I'm Kyle. Great to meet you in person."*
- **Small talk for 2-3 minutes** before looking at anything — neighborhood, how long they've lived there. Be a person first.

---

## 🔍 The Assessment — Room by Room

### 🏗️ Exterior

- [ ] **Roof** — Age, missing shingles, sagging, visible damage, gutters
- [ ] **Siding / Exterior walls** — Cracks, rot, peeling paint
- [ ] **Foundation** — Cracks, settling, water staining, bowing
- [ ] **Windows** — Single/double pane, broken seals, rot around frames
- [ ] **Driveway / Walkways** — Cracks, heaving
- [ ] **Yard / Landscaping** — Overgrown, drainage issues, fencing
- [ ] **Garage / Outbuildings** — Condition, door function

---

### 🍳 Kitchen

- [ ] **Cabinets** — Condition, style, number
- [ ] **Countertops** — Material, condition
- [ ] **Appliances** — What stays, what works, age
- [ ] **Plumbing** — Check under sink for leaks, water pressure

---

### 🚿 Bathrooms

- [ ] **Fixtures** — Toilet, sink, tub/shower condition
- [ ] **Tile** — Cracked, missing, mold/mildew
- [ ] **Plumbing** — Leaks, water pressure, drainage
- [ ] **Ventilation** — Fan present and working?

---

### 🔧 Every Room

- [ ] **Floors** — Type (hardwood, tile, carpet, vinyl), condition, damage
- [ ] **Walls / Ceilings** — Cracks, water stains, holes
- [ ] **Doors / Trim** — Condition, function, hardware
- [ ] **Windows** — Open/close properly

---

### ⚡ Systems (Big Ticket Items)

| System | What to Check |
|---|---|
| **HVAC** | Age, type, does it run? Check unit outside + air handler inside |
| **Water Heater** | Age, type, condition (check label for date) |
| **Electrical Panel** | Updated or old? Fuses vs breakers? Capacity? |
| **Plumbing** | Copper, PEX, galvanized, polybutylene? Known issues? |
| **Sewer** | Ask about backups. Check for belly/sag if possible. |

---

### 🕳️ Basement / Crawlspace / Attic

- [ ] **Moisture / Water intrusion** — Staining, mold, standing water
- [ ] **Insulation** — Present, condition
- [ ] **Structure** — Joists, beams, visible damage
- [ ] **Pests** — Signs of termites, rodents

---

## 📸 Photo Checklist

Take photos of **EVERYTHING.** You can never have too many.

| Area | Shots Needed |
|---|---|
| Front of house | Straight on + angles |
| Back + each side | Full exterior coverage |
| Every room | Stand in doorway, get full room |
| Kitchen | Cabinets, counters, appliances |
| Every bathroom | Full view + close-ups of issues |
| Any damage | Close-up shots |
| HVAC unit | Inside and outside |
| Water heater | Including label with age |
| Electrical panel | Open if possible |
| Roof | From ground level |
| Foundation issues | Close-ups |
| Basement / Crawlspace | Any access points |

💡 **Pro tip:** Take a **10-second video walkthrough** of each room. Faster than individual photos and you can pull frames later.

## 💲 Repair Estimate Ranges

Quick mental math during the walkthrough:

|---|---|
| Roof replacement | $7,000 – $15,000 |
| HVAC replacement | $5,000 – $10,000 |
| Water heater | $1,000 – $2,500 |
| Kitchen remodel (basic) | $8,000 – $20,000 |
| Bathroom remodel (basic) | $3,000 – $8,000 |

| Electrical update | $3,000 – $10,000 |
| Plumbing repipe | $4,000 – $10,000 |

## 🤝 Rapport Building During the Walkthrough

- **Ask about the house's history:** *"How long have you lived here?"* / *"What was it like when you first moved in?"*
- **Compliment something genuine:** *"This backyard is great"* / *"These hardwood floors are beautiful under here"*
- **Be honest about what you see:** *"Yeah, this roof is going to need to be replaced. That's a big expense."*
- **Don't trash the house.** Point out issues **factually**, not judgmentally. It's still their home.
- **Listen more than you talk.** Sellers share more motivation during walkthroughs than on the phone.

---

## ✅ After the Walkthrough

- [ ] Thank the seller and set expectations: *"I'll put the numbers together and have an offer to you by [specific time]."*
- [ ] Log everything in GHL — detailed notes, repair estimates, seller sentiment
- [ ] Upload photos to the contact record
- [ ] Run your numbers: **ARV - Repairs - Holding Costs - Desired Profit = Max Offer**
- [ ] Present the offer within **24 hours** (sooner is better)

---

## 📋 Quick Reference

1. **Two goals: assess the property AND build rapport** — both matter equally
2. **Photograph everything** — you can never have too many photos
3. **Check the big-ticket systems** — roof, HVAC, foundation, plumbing, electrical
4. **Be honest but not judgmental** — point out issues factually, don't trash their home
5. **Present the offer within 24 hours** — speed builds trust and momentum
