# 🔄 Follow-Up Cadence Guide for Lead Managers

> Most deals don't close on the first call. They close on the **3rd, 5th, or 7th touch.** The fortune is in the follow-up — but only if you do it with **structure, not randomness.**

---

## 📞 Standard Cadence: New Leads (No Answer)

| Attempt | Timing | Channel | What to Do |
|---|---|---|---|
| **1** | Day 1 | Call + VM | Intro voicemail — who you are, why you're calling |
| **2** | Day 1 | Text | *"Hi [Name], this is [You] from New Again Houses. Tried to reach you about [Address]. Give me a call — [number]"* |
| **3** | Day 2 | Call | No voicemail — just ring and move on |
| **4** | Day 4 | Call + VM | Different angle — *"Just following up, wanted to see if you had any questions"* |
| **5** | Day 7 | Call + Text | *"Hey [Name], I've tried a few times about [Address]. No pressure — let me know if you'd like to chat or if I should close your file."* |
| **6** | Day 14 | Call | Final attempt before long-term follow-up |
| **7** | Day 30 | Text | Breakup text (see below) |

**After 7 attempts with no contact →** Move to **1 Month Follow Up** stage in GHL.

---

## 💬 The Breakup Text (Attempt 7)

> *"Hey [Name], I've been trying to connect about [Address] but haven't been able to reach you. I don't want to be a bother — if selling isn't on your radar right now, no worries at all. I'll close out your file on my end. If anything changes down the road, you've got my number. Best, [Your Name]"*

💡 **Why this works:** It creates a "closing the loop" moment. People who were ignoring you will often **reply when they feel the opportunity going away.**

## 🎯 Scenario-Based Follow-Up

### Scenario 1: "Call Me Back Later"

Seller answered, interested but not ready to talk.

| Attempt | Timing | What to Say |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | When they said | *"Hey [Name], it's [You] — you asked me to call back today. Got a few minutes?"* |
| 2 | +2 days | *"Following up from our last chat. Still interested in getting you that info on [Address]."* |
| 3 | +5 days | Call + text: *"Checking in — want to make sure I'm not dropping the ball on my end."* |

---

### Scenario 2: Interested But Not Ready

Engaged, shared motivation, but said "not right now."

**Cadence:** Every 2-4 weeks. Stay top of mind without being annoying.

> **Week 2:** *"Hey [Name], just wanted to check in. No pressure — just making sure nothing's changed on your end with the property."*
>
> **Week 4:** *"Hi [Name], touching base. If your situation has changed or you have any questions, I'm here."*
>
> **Week 8:** *"Hey [Name], it's been a couple months since we talked about [Address]. Just wanted to see where things stand. Are you any closer to making a decision?"*

💡 Each follow-up should feel like **you care,** not like you're working a list.

---

### Scenario 3: Went Cold (Was Hot, Stopped Responding)

Was engaged, maybe had a walkthrough scheduled, then ghosted.

| Attempt | Timing | What to Say |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | +2 days | *"Hey [Name], wanted to make sure everything's okay. We were chatting about [Address] and I want to make sure I'm not missing you."* |
| 2 | +5 days | *"Following up one more time. I know life gets busy — just want to make sure you've got everything you need."* |
| 3 | +10 days | *"Hey [Name], I'm going to assume the timing isn't right and close your file for now. If things change, my number's saved."* |

---

### Scenario 4: Appointment No-Show

Seller was scheduled for a walkthrough but didn't show.

| Timing | What to Do |
|---|---|
| **Same day** (within 2 hours) | *"Hey [Name], Kyle was heading out to [Address] today — looks like we might have missed each other. Want to reschedule?"* |
| **+1 day** | *"No worries about yesterday — things happen. When's a good time to reschedule the walkthrough?"* |
| **+3 days** | *"Checking in one more time about rescheduling. Let me know what works and I'll set it up."* |

## 📆 Long-Term Follow-Up Stages (GHL Pipeline)

### 1 Month Follow Up

- Attempt contact once
- Text: *"Hey [Name], it's [You] from New Again Houses. Just checking in on [Address] — anything changed since we last talked?"*
- No response → Move to **4 Month Follow Up**

### 4 Month Follow Up

- Attempt contact once
- Text: *"Hi [Name], it's been a few months. Wanted to see if you've made any decisions about [Address]. We're still buying in your area if the timing's better now."*
- No response → Move to **1 Year Follow Up**

### 1 Year Follow Up

- Attempt contact once
- Text: *"Hey [Name], it's been a while! Just checking if [Address] is still something you're holding onto or if you'd ever consider an offer. Either way, hope you're doing well."*
- No response → **Archive**

---

## 📝 What to Track in GHL

Every follow-up attempt gets logged with:

- ✅ **Date and time** of the attempt
- ✅ **Channel** (call, text, voicemail)
- ✅ **Outcome** (no answer, left VM, spoke briefly, scheduled callback, etc.)
- ✅ **Notes** on anything the seller said
- ✅ **Next action** scheduled

**If it's not in GHL, it didn't happen.**

---

## 📋 Quick Reference

1. **7 attempts before long-term follow-up** — structured, not random
2. **The breakup text works** — people respond when they feel the window closing
3. **Match your cadence to the scenario** — hot leads get faster follow-up than cold ones
4. **Every attempt gets logged in GHL** — date, channel, outcome, next step
5. **Follow-up is service, not stalking** — you're making sure they don't miss an opportunity
