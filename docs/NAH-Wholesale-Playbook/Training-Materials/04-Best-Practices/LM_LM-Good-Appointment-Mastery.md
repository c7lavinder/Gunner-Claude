# LM Good Appointment Mastery

**Role:** Lead Manager
**Category:** Best Practices

> How to Set Up a Successful Appointment

---

Criteria of a Good Appointment 
Has True Motivation- 
There is a difference between motivation to sell, and true motivation. We want to find the true motivation in sellers. 
Example 1: A seller says “Yes I want to sell because I know the market is hot.”. That is motivation to sell, but not true motivation to sell at a discounted price to investors like us. 
Example 2: A seller says, “I wasn’t looking to sell but you contacted me.”
True motivation comes when there is a problem that needs to be solved. It is your job to decide if their motivation is just listening to cash offers because people are calling them, or true motivation where they need to sell for quick cash and at a discounted price. 
Example 1: Probate- The property got passed down to them through a deceased family member, and had to go through attorneys to have seller's name on the deed and have right to sell
Example 2: Divorce- Just finished the divorce process, and looking to move on from the home because they are no longer together 
Example 3: Tenants- bad tenant stories from the past, that makes them no longer want to be a landlord (ex. Not buying rent, squatters, trashed the house, etc.)
As a Lead Manager, Motivation is the number 1 most important thing that you set up your appointments on. Pulling out pain and motivation is how we get deeper discounts on properties. The more motivation, usually creates more urgency to get the deal done. 
Discounted Price-  
	Buying properties for cash at market value is something we don’t do.
 We want to talk to sellers that are willing to compromise a little bit of money for our speed and convenience of buying the property cash, as is, and closing on their timeline.
 The more motivation you can pull out, the more of a discounted price you will get.
Example 1: “I need to just get what I owe on this property so I can be done with it and move on” 
Example 2: “I know the property is worth 200k on the market, but I don’t have time or energy to fix it up so if you can just get me close to 100k I would be ready to move forward”
 There isn’t room for us to make money off of a deal unless it’s discount is well below market value because we have to sell it for more than our contract price with the seller.
Example 1: A discounted price is NOT when the seller says “I will knock off 10k for you since I don’t have to pay any commissions”

WALKTHROUGH APT
Ready to say Yes or No and Able to meet us at house in next 48 hours 
Up Front Contract/Setting Expectations- The last part of what makes a good appointment is having the seller agree to what we call an “upfront contract” or also known as setting expectations.
 We need the seller to agree to two things before we set the appointment with the franchise owner.
They can give a confident yes or no to our offer once we walk the house
All of the decision makers can be together for the offer call after apt
Why are those two things important? 
We need the sellers to agree to give us a confident yes or no once the offer is made because we have found if we give it to them and let them think on it then they will either-
 take that number and shop it around to other investors OR 
they will never call us back because they are too afraid to tell us no 
The same thing will happen if all decision makers are not present
Example:  the husband and wife are both on the deed but both are not on the call, then the husband might say yes to us but say he needs to go talk to his wife. If he does that and she doesn’t like it then we might never hear from them again. 
We are spending a lot of time to go walk the house. If we are doing that they need to respect our time and get us a yes or no. 
The very last thing we want is for a seller to ghost us after we give them an offer which is why these two things are crucial.
