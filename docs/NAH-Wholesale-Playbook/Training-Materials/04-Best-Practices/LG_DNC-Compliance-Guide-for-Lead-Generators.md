# 🚨 DNC Compliance Guide for Lead Generators

> DNC violations can result in fines of **$500–$50,000+ per call.** This isn't optional. This is the law. Follow these rules every single time, no exceptions.

---

## 📖 The Basics

The **National Do Not Call Registry** is a list of phone numbers from people who don't want telemarketing calls. It's managed by the FTC (Federal Trade Commission).

**Our obligations:**

- ✅ Maintain an **internal DNC list** — anyone who asks us to stop calling gets added immediately
- ✅ **Scrub call lists** against the National DNC Registry before dialing
- ✅ **Honor removal requests instantly** — the moment someone says "stop calling," they're off
- ✅ **Keep records** — document every DNC request with date, time, and phone number

---

## ✅ What You CAN Do

- Call numbers that are **NOT** on the National DNC Registry
- Call people with an **existing business relationship** (they contacted us, we bought from them before)
- Call numbers that have been **properly scrubbed and cleared**
- Ask **one polite redirect question** before honoring a removal request
- Leave **voicemails** (with your name, company name, and callback number)

---

## ❌ What You CANNOT Do

- Call numbers **on the National DNC Registry** (unless existing relationship)
- **Ignore** a "take me off your list" request — ever
- **Call someone back** after they've asked to be removed
- Use **fake caller ID** or misrepresent who you are
- Call **before 8:00 AM or after 9:00 PM** in the seller's time zone
- Use **threatening or harassing language**

---

## 🎯 How to Handle "Take Me Off Your List"

This is the most common DNC situation. Here's exactly how to handle it:

### Step 1: Acknowledge Immediately

> **You:** "Absolutely, I can take care of that for you."

### Step 2: One Soft Redirect (Optional)

You get **ONE** chance to see if they have a property situation:

> **You:** "Before I do — just so I note it correctly — is this a property you're keeping long-term, or was it more of a timing thing?"

### Step 3: If They Insist — Stop. Immediately.

> **You:** "No problem at all. I'm removing your number right now. You won't hear from us again. Have a good day."

**That's it. No guilt trips. No "are you sure?" No calling back in a month.**

### Step 4: Document and Remove

1. **In your dialer:** Mark the number as DNC
2. **In GHL:** Add a `DNC` tag to the contact. Add a note: *"Requested DNC on [date]. Removed from all call lists."*
3. **Internal DNC list:** Make sure the number is added

---

## ⚠️ "Stop Calling" vs. "Not Interested"

These are **DIFFERENT:**

| What They Say | What It Means | What You Do |
|---|---|---|
| "Take me off your list" | 🚨 DNC request | Remove immediately, document |
| "Stop calling me" | 🚨 DNC request | Remove immediately, document |
| "Don't ever call me again" | 🚨 DNC request | Remove immediately, document |
| "I'm not interested" | Brush-off, **not** DNC | One redirect attempt, then move on |
| "Not right now" | Timing issue | Schedule follow-up |
| "No thanks" | Soft decline | One redirect, then move on |

💡 **When in doubt, treat it as a DNC request.** Better to lose one lead than get fined.

## 🕐 Calling Hours

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Earliest** | 8:00 AM in the **seller's** time zone |
| **Latest** | 9:00 PM in the **seller's** time zone |
| **Nashville** | Central Time — but adjust for other time zones |

---

## 📞 Caller ID Rules

- Always display a **real, working callback number**
- **Never spoof** or fake your caller ID
- If a seller calls back, someone should answer or they should reach a professional voicemail

---

## 💰 What Happens If We Violate DNC Rules

| Violation | Consequence |
|---|---|
| First violation | **$500+ fine** per call |
| Willful violations | **Up to $50,000+** per call |
| Repeat violations | Lawsuits, company shutdown, personal liability |
| Reputation | Damages trust with every seller we talk to |

---

## 📁 Record Keeping

We're required to keep DNC records for **5 years.** This includes:

- Our internal DNC list
- Date and time of each DNC request
- Who made the request
- Who took the request

**This is why logging in GHL is non-negotiable.** Every DNC interaction gets documented.

## 📋 Quick Reference

1. **"Take me off your list" = immediate removal** — no arguing, no second chances

4. **When in doubt, treat it as DNC** — losing a lead beats a fine
5. **One soft redirect is okay** — but if they repeat the request, stop immediately
