# 📅 Appointment Setting Guide for Lead Managers

> Every call with a motivated seller must end with a **next step.** Not "reach out whenever you're ready." Not "we'll be here." A **specific, scheduled next action.**

---

## 🚨 The Rule: Never Empathy + Goodbye

| ✅ Good | ❌ Bad |
|---|---|
| Empathy **+ Next Step** | Empathy **+ Goodbye** |
| "I'm so sorry. Let me call you March 3rd." | "I'll be on standby. Reach out anytime." |

If you show empathy but don't set a next step, you just had a nice conversation **that goes nowhere.**

---

## 🎬 The Robin Phelps Lesson

**This actually happened on our team.**

A seller named Robin Phelps called in. Her mother was on hospice. She had a property she needed to deal with but said she wouldn't be back in town until March. She was emotional, overwhelmed, and not ready to make decisions.

The Lead Manager responded with genuine empathy — the right move. But then he ended with:

> **LM:** "I'll be on standby. Whenever you're ready, just reach out and we'll be here for you."

**What happened:** Robin never called back. Life got in the way. Her mother's situation consumed her. We lost the deal — not because she wasn't motivated, but because **we didn't set the next step.**

### ✅ What Should Have Been Said:

> **You:** "Robin, I'm so sorry about your mom. I can't imagine what you're going through right now. You mentioned you'll be back in town in March — how about I give you a call the first week of March? That way you don't have to worry about remembering to call us. I'll reach out, and if the timing's right, we'll go from there. If things change before then, you've got my number. Does the first week of March work?"

**Same empathy. Same compassion. But now there's a scheduled callback.** Robin doesn't have to remember — we call her.

## 🎯 How to Set Appointments — Step by Step

### Step 1: Summarize What You've Learned

> **You:** "So just to make sure I've got this right — you've got the property on Oak Street, it's been vacant since your dad passed, it needs a new roof and some foundation work, and you'd like to get it handled sooner rather than later. Sound right?"

### Step 2: Propose the Next Step

> **You:** "Here's what I'd suggest — let me have Kyle, our acquisitions manager, come take a look at the property. He'll walk through it, assess the condition, and put together a real number for you. No obligation, no pressure. He's usually out in your area on [Day]. Would [Day] morning or afternoon work better?"

### Step 3: Use the Either/Or Close

Don't ask "when are you free?" — that's too open-ended. **Give two options:**

- "Would **Tuesday or Thursday** work better?"
- "**Morning or afternoon?**"
- "**This week or next?**"

Two choices. Both lead to an appointment.

### Step 4: Confirm the Details

> **You:** "Perfect — so Kyle will be at 412 Oak Street on Thursday at 2 PM. He'll call you about 15 minutes before to confirm. It usually takes about 20-30 minutes. Sound good?"

### Step 5: Set Expectations

> **You:** "After the walkthrough, Kyle will put together an offer based on the condition. He can usually have that to you within 24 hours. No pressure to accept — just gives you a real number to work with."

## 🛡️ Handling Pushback on Appointments

### "I'm not ready yet."

> **You:** "That's totally fine. You don't have to commit to anything — the walkthrough is just so we can see the property and give you a real number. Think of it as getting information so you can make the best decision when you ARE ready. Would next week work to at least get that done?"

---

### "I need to talk to my spouse/family first."

> **You:** "Of course. How about I pencil in a time for later this week, and if anything changes you can let me know? That way we don't lose momentum. Would Thursday or Friday work?"

---

### "Just send me the offer."

> **You:** "I wish I could, but to give you a real, accurate number, we need to see the property. I don't want to throw out a number that isn't fair to you. The walkthrough is quick — 20 minutes — and then you'll have a solid number to work with."

---

### "Call me back in a few weeks/months."

> **You:** "Absolutely. Let me put that on my calendar so I don't forget. Would the [specific date] work? … And just so you know, you can always call me before then if anything changes."

💡 **Always pin a vague timeframe to a specific date.** "A few weeks" → "March 3rd."

---

### "I'll let you know."

> **You:** "Sounds good. Just so I can follow up properly — would it be okay if I check in with you on [specific day]? That way you don't have to worry about remembering to call back."

---

## ✅ The Non-Negotiables

1. **Every call ends with a next step.** Period. Even if it's "I'll call you back on March 3rd."
2. **Use specific dates and times.** "Next week" is not a next step. "Tuesday at 2 PM" is.
3. **Put it in GHL.** Create the appointment, set the task, add the notes. If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.
4. **Confirm everything.** Repeat the date, time, address, and who'll be there.
5. **Send a reminder.** Text or call to confirm the day before or morning of.

---

## 💡 The Mindset

You're not being pushy by setting appointments. You're being **helpful.**

Sellers are dealing with stressful situations — the last thing they need is another thing to remember. When you set the next step, you're **taking something off their plate.**

> "Let me take care of this. I'll call you on the 15th so you don't have to think about it."

That's service, not sales.

---

## 📋 Quick Reference

1. **Never empathy + goodbye** — always empathy + next step
2. **Pin vague timeframes to specific dates** — "a few weeks" becomes "March 3rd at 2 PM"
3. **Use either/or choices** — "Tuesday or Thursday?" not "When are you free?"
4. **Log everything in GHL** — appointment, task, notes, confirmation
5. **Setting the next step IS the service** — you're making their life easier, not being pushy
