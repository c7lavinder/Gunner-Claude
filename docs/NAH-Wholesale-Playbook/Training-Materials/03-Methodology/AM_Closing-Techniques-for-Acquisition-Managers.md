**Category:** Methodology

---

# 🎯 Closing Techniques for Acquisition Managers

> Closing isn't a trick. It's the **natural conclusion** of a good conversation. If you've extracted motivation, built rapport, presented well, and handled objections — closing is just asking for the decision.

---

## ⏰ Creating Urgency Without Pressure

Never pressure a seller into a regretful decision. But you **can** highlight real factors:

### 💸 Carrying Cost Urgency

> **You:** "Every month that goes by, you're paying $[mortgage + taxes + insurance]. That's $[amount] you could save by closing now."

### 📊 Market Urgency

> **You:** "We're buying actively right now, and our budget for this area is based on current pricing. I can't guarantee this number will be the same in 60-90 days."

### 🎯 Situation Urgency (Use Their Motivation)

> **You:** "You mentioned you need to be relocated by [date]. If we start the paperwork this week, we can close well before then."

### 🏠 Competition Urgency (Only If True)

> **You:** "I want to be upfront — we're looking at a couple properties in this area right now. I'd love to make this work for you, but I want to be honest about where we are."

🚨 **Never create fake urgency.** Sellers can smell BS.

---

## 🔑 The Closes

### 1. ✅ The Assumptive Close

Act as if the next step is already decided. Confidence is contagious.

> **You:** "Okay great. Let me get the paperwork drawn up. I'll have it over to you by tomorrow, and we can schedule closing for [date]. Does that work?"

---

### 2. 🔀 The Either/Or Close

Two options — both lead to a yes.

> **You:** "Would you prefer to close in two weeks or would the end of the month work better?"

> **You:** "Do you want us to handle the title company, or do you have an attorney you'd prefer?"

---

### 3. 📝 The Summary Close

Restate everything, then ask for the decision.

> **You:** "So just to recap — we buy the property as-is, no repairs needed. We cover all closing costs, no commissions, close by [date]. You walk away with $[amount], clean and done. Ready to move forward?"

---

### 4. 🤔 The "What Would It Take?" Close

When you're stuck and the seller is on the fence:

> **You:** "Help me understand — what would it take to make this work for you today?"

This often reveals the **real objection** you haven't addressed yet.

---

### 5. 🧪 The Trial Close

Test the waters before full commitment:

> **You:** "If we could close by [date] and get you $[amount], is that something you'd move forward on?"

If yes → close. If no → now you know what to work on.

---

### 6. 🎭 The "Take It Away" Close

Pull back slightly to remove pressure:

> **You:** "Look, I don't want you to do anything you're not 100% comfortable with. If this isn't the right fit, no hard feelings. But if it is, I'd love to get this wrapped up for you this week."

💡 Removing pressure often **triggers commitment** because they don't feel cornered.

---

## 😐 Handling the "Maybe"

"Maybe" is the worst outcome. It's not yes and not no — it just sits there.

### When they say "maybe" or "probably":

> **You:** "I appreciate that. Help me understand — what's the 'maybe' about? Is it the price, the timing, or something else?"

### When they say "I'll call you back":

> **You:** "Sounds good. Rather than leave it open, how about I call you on [specific day/time]? That way it's on my calendar and you don't have to remember."

### When they keep stalling:

> **You:** "I want to respect your time and mine. It seems like something's holding you back. Can we talk about what that is? I'd rather you tell me no than have this drag on."

---

## 🔗 Commitment Language — Building Micro-Yeses

Small verbal commitments build toward the big one:

| Question | Gets a "Yes" |
|---|---|
| "Does that make sense?" | ✅ |
| "Would that work for your timeline?" | ✅ |
| "Is that something you'd be open to?" | ✅ |
| "Can I send over the agreement?" | ✅ |

Each "yes" makes the final "yes" easier.

---

## 🚀 After They Say Yes

**Move fast.** Every hour you wait increases the chance they change their mind.

1. ✅ Confirm details verbally — price, timeline, what's included
2. ✅ *"I'm getting the purchase agreement drawn up right now. I'll have it to you by [time]."*
3. ✅ Send the agreement **ASAP**
4. ✅ Set the closing date and confirm with title company
5. ✅ Follow up next day: *"Just checking in — did you get the agreement? Any questions?"*

---

## 📋 Quick Reference

1. **Closing is asking for the decision, not tricking them** — it should feel natural
2. **Create honest urgency** — carrying costs, timeline, real factors. Never fake it.
3. **Use assumptive and either/or closes** — confidence and choices move people forward
4. **Kill the "maybe"** — dig into what's behind it and get to yes or no
5. **When they say yes, move fast** — send paperwork immediately, confirm everything
