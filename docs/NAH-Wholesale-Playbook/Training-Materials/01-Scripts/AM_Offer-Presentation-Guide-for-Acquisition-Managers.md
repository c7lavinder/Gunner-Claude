# 💰 Offer Presentation Guide for Acquisition Managers

> You're not apologizing for a low number. You're **presenting a solution.** The seller has a problem and you're offering certainty, speed, and simplicity. The price is one part of the equation — your job is to make sure they see the **full picture.**

---

## 🔁 Before the Number: Restate the Motivation

Before any number leaves your mouth, remind the seller **why they're here.**

> **You:** "So [Name], just to make sure I've got this right — you've been dealing with [their situation], and ideally you'd like to [their goal]. Is that right?"

Let them confirm. Now the offer isn't a random number — it's a **response to their specific problem.**

---

## 🎯 The Offer Framework

### Step 1: Lead with Benefits, Not Price

> **You:** "Here's what I can do for you. We'll buy the property exactly as it sits — you don't need to fix anything, clean anything, or move anything you don't want. We cover all the closing costs. There are no agent fees, no commissions. And we can close in as little as two weeks. You literally just sign and walk away."

### Step 2: Deliver the Number with Confidence

> **You:** "Based on the condition of the property, the repairs that are needed, and what similar homes are selling for in the area, we can offer you **$[amount].**"

**Then stop talking.** 🤫

Let the number land. Don't explain it, don't justify it, don't apologize. Just pause.

### Step 3: Let Them React

The first person who speaks after the number loses leverage. **Stay quiet.**

| Their Reaction | What It Means | Your Move |
|---|---|---|
| **Silence** | They're thinking | Wait. Don't fill it. |
| "That's lower than I expected" | Normal pushback | Move to reframing |
| "I was hoping for more" | Negotiable | Ask how much more |
| "That could work" | ✅ Ready | Move to next steps immediately |
| "No way" | Big gap | Ask what they had in mind |

---

## 🔄 Reframing the Value

When a seller pushes back on price, don't just negotiate the number — **reframe what they're getting.**

### The Value Stack

> **You:** "I totally understand that's not what you'd get if you listed on the market. But here's what that route looks like:
>
> - **Repairs to get show-ready:** $[X]
> - **Agent commissions (6%):** $[X]
> - **Closing costs on your side:** $[X]
> - **3-6 months waiting** = $[X] more in mortgage, insurance, taxes
>
> When you add all that up, our offer nets you pretty close to what you'd walk away with anyway — but you get it in **two weeks** with **zero hassle.**"

### The Time Value

> **You:** "Every month that property sits, you're paying $[monthly cost]. That's $[annual cost] a year. Our offer gets you out from under that **starting now.**"

---

## 🏠 At the Property vs. Over the Phone

### ✅ At the Property (Preferred)

- Walk through together. Point out the issues you'll be fixing.
- Be conversational: *"See this foundation crack? That's probably a $10K fix for us."*
- Build the repair narrative as you go — so by the time you sit down for the number, **they understand why.**
- Present in a quiet, seated setting — kitchen table, living room. Not standing in the driveway.

### 📞 Over the Phone

- Send a follow-up email or text with the offer in writing **immediately** after.
- Include a simple breakdown of benefits.
- Be extra clear on next steps:

> **You:** "I'm sending this over now. Take a look, talk it over if you need to, and I'll give you a call tomorrow to see where you're at."

---

## ✅ The Close After the Offer

Don't present and just wait forever. Guide them toward a decision:

> **You:** "I know this is a big decision. Here's what I'd suggest — take the rest of today to think about it, talk to whoever you need to talk to, and I'll give you a call tomorrow afternoon. If it works, we can get the paperwork started and have you closed in two weeks. Sound fair?"

---

## 📋 Quick Reference

1. **Restate motivation before the number** — connect the offer to their problem
2. **Lead with benefits: as-is, no fees, fast close** — price is just one part
3. **Say the number and stop talking** — let them react first
4. **Reframe, don't just negotiate** — show the true cost of their alternatives
5. **Always set a next step after the offer** — "I'll call you tomorrow afternoon"
