## The Hiring Journey

Scaling a wholesaling operation follows a predictable path. Each revenue milestone unlocks the next key hire, and each key hire unlocks the next revenue milestone.

| Revenue | Team Size | Key Hire | What Changes |
|---------|-----------|----------|-------------|
| $0 – First Deal | 1 | — | You do everything |
| $15K/mo | 1–2 | **Lead Manager** | Follow-up actually happens consistently |
| $30K/mo | 2–4 | TC / Admin / EA | Administrative work comes off your plate |
| $50K/mo | 3–6 | **Acquisition Manager** | This is the most important hire |
| $100K/mo+ | 6–10+ | 2nd AM, Dispo, Marketing | Stabilize and grow |
| $300K/mo | ~28 | Full departments | You become CEO only |

The Acquisition Manager hire at $50K/month is the inflection point. Teams that make this hire correctly see immediate results:

> "They IMMEDIATELY blow up to $100K."

## Profit First (Non-Negotiable)

Revenue without profit is a job with worse hours. The Profit First framework ensures the business is actually profitable, not just busy.

| Bucket | Percentage of Revenue |
|--------|----------------------|
| Profit | 10–15% (taken FIRST) |
| Owner's Pay | 30–40% |
| Tax | 15–20% |
| Operating Expenses | 30–40% |

The critical principle: profit is taken FIRST, not last. Most wholesalers calculate profit as "whatever is left after expenses." This guarantees that expenses expand to consume all revenue. By taking profit first, you force the business to operate within its means.

> "Most wholesalers do $50K months but keep $5K. That's not a business — that's a job with worse hours."

## KPIs That Matter

### Marketing KPIs
| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|------------------|
| Cost per lead | Raw acquisition cost |
| Cost per qualified lead | Quality-adjusted acquisition cost |
| Cost per contract | True cost of a signed deal |
| Cost per closed deal | End-to-end marketing efficiency |
| ROI by channel | Which marketing source is actually profitable |

### Sales KPIs
| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|------------------|
| Speed to lead | How fast you're responding to new leads |
| Calls per day | Activity volume |
| Conversations per day | Meaningful contact rate |
| Appointments set | Pipeline generation |
| Offers made | Closing activity |
| Contracts signed | Revenue generation |
| Follow-up rate | Lead nurture discipline |

### Financial KPIs
| Metric | What It Tells You |
|--------|------------------|
| Gross revenue | Top-line performance |
| Marketing spend | Investment level |
| Net profit | Actual money kept |
| Profit per deal | Deal quality |
| Profit margin | Business health |

## Red Flags

These patterns indicate a scaling problem that will eventually break the business:

- **Marketing spend exceeds 40% of revenue** — The business is buying revenue, not earning it
- **2+ marketing channels without per-channel ROI tracking** — You don't know which channel is profitable and which is bleeding money
- **Hiring ahead of revenue** — Adding headcount before the revenue supports it creates a cash flow crisis
- **Revenue growing but profit flat** — The business is scaling expenses faster than income

## The ONE Channel Rule

This principle is unanimously endorsed across the industry:

> "I see this over and over — wholesalers throwing money at multiple channels with zero focus, zero measurement, zero consistency."

> "The biggest mistake is spending on 10 marketing sources."

The approach is simple: Pick ONE marketing channel. Master it. Scale it until it's maxed out. Only THEN add a second channel.

Spreading budget and attention across multiple unproven channels guarantees mediocre results everywhere. Focused effort on a single channel produces mastery, optimization, and predictable returns.

## The Daily Optimizer

For operators and managers, the time audit is the most powerful scaling tool:

1. List every task that consumes your attention during a workday
2. Grade each task 0–3 based on revenue impact
3. Tasks graded 0 and 1 → delegate or automate immediately
4. Tasks graded 2 and 3 → invest more time in these

> "4% of activities produce 64% of your outcomes."

The operator's job is to ruthlessly eliminate low-value activities and protect time for high-value ones. This is how you go from "doing everything" to "running the business."
