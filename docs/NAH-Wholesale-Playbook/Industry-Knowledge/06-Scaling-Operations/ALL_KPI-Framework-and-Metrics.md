# KPI Framework and Metrics for Wholesale Operations

**Applicable To:** All Roles
**Source:** Chandler Saine (Level Up REI) + Courtney Scott (Alpha Media) — KPIs, Marketing ROI, Financial Health

---

## Why KPIs Matter

> "Your first $100K month is a systems problem, not a marketing problem. Most businesses have enough leads. They just waste them."

Without measurement, there is no management. KPIs transform a wholesaling operation from a series of gut decisions into a data-driven business. Every dollar spent, every call made, and every deal closed should be traceable through the metrics framework.

---

## Marketing KPIs

These metrics tell you whether your lead generation is working and where to invest more (or less).

| Metric | Definition | Target Range | Why It Matters |
|--------|-----------|-------------|---------------|
| **Cost per lead (CPL)** | Total marketing spend / total leads generated | $5–50 (Meta), varies by channel | Raw acquisition cost — are you overpaying for attention? |
| **Cost per qualified lead** | Total marketing spend / qualified leads | $20–100 | Quality-adjusted cost — are you attracting the right sellers? |
| **Cost per contract** | Total marketing spend / signed contracts | $500–3,000 | True cost of a signed deal |
| **Cost per closed deal** | Total marketing spend / closed deals | Varies | End-to-end marketing efficiency including fallout |
| **ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** | Revenue from deals / marketing spend | 3–10x+ | The ultimate marketing metric |
| **Landing page conversion rate** | Form submissions / page visitors | 10%+ | Funnel efficiency — fix this before increasing traffic |
| **ROI by channel** | Per-channel revenue / per-channel spend | Positive | Which channel is actually profitable? |

> "Conversion rate is greater than traffic. $10 CPC at 1% = $1,000/lead. At 10% = $100/lead. Fix the landing page FIRST."

### The ONE Channel Rule

Before tracking ROI across multiple channels, master one channel first.

> "The biggest mistake is spending on 10 marketing sources."

Pick one channel. Track every metric above for that channel. Optimize until it is profitable and scalable. Only then add a second channel and begin comparative ROI tracking.

## Sales KPIs

These metrics tell you whether your team is converting leads into deals efficiently.

| Metric | Definition | Who Owns It | Target |
|--------|-----------|------------|--------|
| **Speed to lead** | Time from lead creation to first human contact | LM / System | Under 5 minutes |
| **Calls per day** | Total outbound dials | LG / LM | 1,000+ (cold), 50+ (warm follow-up) |
| **Conversations per day** | Calls that result in meaningful dialogue | LG / LM | 10+ from cold calling |
| **Appointments set** | Qualified leads scheduled for AM call or visit | LM | Varies by volume |
| **Offers made** | Formal offers presented to sellers | AM | Track weekly |
| **Contracts signed** | Purchase agreements executed | AM | Track weekly |
| **Follow-up rate** | Percentage of leads receiving timely follow-up | LM | 100% within cadence |
| **Conversion rate (lead → contract)** | Contracts / total leads | Team | 2–5% |
| **Conversion rate (appointment → contract)** | Contracts / appointments | AM | 20–40% |

### Speed to Lead

This is the single most impactful sales KPI. Research consistently shows that the probability of qualifying a lead drops dramatically after the first 5 minutes.

> "Attack leads like a rabid dog."

Auto-SMS should fire within seconds of lead creation. The first human call should happen within 5 minutes. Every minute of delay reduces conversion probability.

## Financial KPIs

These metrics tell you whether the business is actually making money — not just generating revenue.

| Metric | Definition | Healthy Range |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| **Gross revenue** | Total assignment fees and deal proceeds | Growth trend |
| **Marketing spend** | Total investment in lead generation | Under 40% of revenue |
| **Net profit** | Revenue minus all expenses | Positive and growing |
| **Profit per deal** | Average net profit per closed transaction | $10K–25K+ |
| **Profit margin** | Net profit / gross revenue | 20–40% |
| **Owner's compensation** | What the owner actually takes home | 30–40% of revenue (Profit First) |

### The Profit First Framework

Revenue allocation should follow this structure:

| Bucket | Percentage | Rule |
|--------|-----------|------|
| **Profit** | 10–15% | Taken FIRST, not last |
| **Owner's Pay** | 30–40% | Consistent regardless of revenue fluctuations |
| **Tax Reserve** | 15–20% | Set aside before spending |
| **Operating Expenses** | 30–40% | Everything else |

> "Most wholesalers do $50K months but keep $5K. That's not a business — that's a job with worse hours."

## Role-Based KPI Views

Different team members need different metrics. Showing everyone everything creates noise. Show each role only what they can influence.

### Lead Generator View
- Dials per day
- Conversations per day
- Leads generated
- Cost per lead (if running ads)

### Lead Manager View
- Speed to lead
- Follow-up compliance rate
- Leads qualified and passed to AM
- Appointments set
- Lead temperature distribution

### Acquisition Manager View
- Appointments attended
- Offers made
- Contracts signed
- Average assignment fee
- Conversion rate (appointment → contract)
- Call grades and coaching scores

### Owner / Operator View
- All financial KPIs
- Marketing ROI by channel
- Team performance comparison
- Pipeline value
- Profit margin trend

## Red Flags and Warning Signs

These patterns in KPI data indicate problems that need immediate attention:

| Red Flag | What It Means | Action Required |
|----------|--------------|----------------|
| High CPL but low cost per contract | Marketing is expensive but effective | Acceptable — optimize CPL over time |
| Low CPL but high cost per contract | Cheap leads but poor quality | Fix targeting or qualification criteria |
| Marketing spend exceeds 40% of revenue | Buying revenue instead of earning it | Cut underperforming channels |
| Speed to lead over 30 minutes | Leads are going cold before contact | Fix automation or staffing |
| Follow-up rate below 80% | Leads are falling through cracks | CRM discipline, LM accountability |
| Offers made but no contracts | Pricing or negotiation problem | Review AM calls, adjust offer formula |
| Revenue growing but profit flat | Expenses scaling faster than income | Audit expenses, apply Profit First |
| Single AM doing 80% of deals | Key person risk | Hire and train second AM |

## Tracking Cadence

| Frequency | What to Review |
|-----------|---------------|
| **Daily** | Calls made, conversations, speed to lead, appointments set |
| **Weekly** | Offers made, contracts signed, pipeline value, follow-up compliance |
| **Monthly** | Marketing ROI by channel, profit margin, cost per deal, team comparison |
| **Quarterly** | Channel performance review, hiring decisions, Profit First audit |
