# Cold Call Objection Responses

**Applicable To:** Lead Generator
**Source:** Tyson Smith (Virtual Flip Formula) — Cold Calling Scripts, Objection Handling, Volume Mindset

---

## Context

Cold call objections are fundamentally different from closing objections. On a cold call, the seller did not ask to speak with you. They are surprised, possibly annoyed, and their default response is to end the conversation as quickly as possible. The goal is not to close the deal on this call — it is to keep the conversation going long enough to determine if there is motivation.

> "99% of cold calling is just finding people interested in a cash offer."

---

## The Core Objections and Responses

### "How did you get my number?"

This is the most common first objection. It is not a real objection — it is a reflex. The seller is startled and wants to understand the situation before engaging.

**Response:** "My partner compiled a list of areas we want to buy in. Your property came up as one we're interested in."

Keep it simple and honest. Do not over-explain. Do not apologize. The tone should be casual and matter-of-fact, as if calling property owners is the most normal thing in the world — because for you, it is.

### "What's your offer?"

The seller wants a number before giving you any information. This is a trap. If you give a number too early, it will either be too low (and they hang up) or too high (and you lose money).

**Response — The Car Analogy:** "Have you ever test driven a car before buying it? Same thing here — I need to see the property first before I can give you a fair number. When would be a good time to take a look?"

This analogy works because it is relatable, non-confrontational, and logically sound. Everyone understands that you cannot price something you have not seen.

### "I don't know if I want to sell"

This is the most important objection to handle correctly. Many callers hear this and move on. That is a mistake.

**Response:** "Before I called, were you even considering it?"

If the answer is no — genuinely no — move on. But if there is any hesitation, any "well, maybe," any "it depends on the price," you have a lead. Most sellers who say "I don't know" are actually saying "convince me it's worth my time."

> "80% of 'unmotivated' sellers ARE motivated."

### "I'm not interested"

This is the fastest dismissal. It usually comes within the first 10 seconds.

**Response:** "Totally understand. Quick question before I let you go — is the property something you'd consider selling in the next 6–12 months if the price was right?"

This reframes the conversation from "right now" to "eventually." Many sellers who are not interested today will be interested in 6 months. Getting that acknowledgment turns a dead call into a follow-up lead.

### "How much are you offering?"

Similar to "What's your offer?" but more direct and sometimes more aggressive.

**Response:** "I wish I could give you a number right now, but I'd be guessing and that wouldn't be fair to either of us. Can I ask you a couple quick questions about the property so I can get you an accurate number?"

This positions you as fair and thorough rather than evasive. The seller respects that you are not throwing out random numbers.

### "Send me something in writing"

This is a stall tactic. The seller wants to end the call without saying no.

**Response:** "Absolutely, I can do that. What's the best email? And just so I can send you something relevant — what's the general condition of the property?"

By agreeing to their request AND asking a qualifying question, you keep the conversation going while respecting their preference. The qualifying question often opens up a longer conversation.

### "I already have a Realtor"

The seller believes listing with an agent is their best option.

**Response:** "That's great. How long has it been on the market? ... If it doesn't sell in the next [X] weeks, would you be open to a backup offer from us?"

This is not competitive — it is complementary. You are positioning yourself as the safety net, not the replacement. Many listed properties expire, and when they do, you want to be the first call.

## The Volume Mindset

Every objection is expected. Every "no" is normal. The math is simple:

> "Every cold call is spinning a roulette board. Most say no. But there are slivers that say $10K, $50K, $100K. If you could spin for free all day, you'd never leave."

The emotional discipline required for cold calling is not about handling individual objections perfectly. It is about maintaining energy and positivity across hundreds of calls per day, knowing that the vast majority will be rejections.

**Minimum daily volume:** 1,000+ dials per day. At this volume, even a 1% conversation rate produces 10 meaningful conversations. Even a 1% conversion from conversations to deals produces consistent monthly closings.

**Average timeline to first deal:** 2.5 months of consistent daily calling.

---

## Tone and Energy

> "Fight fire with WATER — calm beats aggressive every time."

The single most important factor in cold calling is not what you say — it is how you say it. A calm, friendly, conversational tone outperforms a scripted, aggressive, or overly enthusiastic tone every time.

The seller can hear desperation. They can hear scripts being read. They can hear fake enthusiasm. What they respond to is a real person having a real conversation.

**Key principles:**
- Speak at a normal pace, not fast
- Use the seller's name
- Smile while talking (it changes your voice)
- If they're angry, get calmer — not louder
- If they're curious, match their energy
- If they're short, be shorter

> "Once you taste blood, you're never going back."

The first deal changes everything. The objections stop feeling personal. The rejections stop hurting. The volume becomes automatic. Everything before the first deal is an investment. Everything after is momentum.
