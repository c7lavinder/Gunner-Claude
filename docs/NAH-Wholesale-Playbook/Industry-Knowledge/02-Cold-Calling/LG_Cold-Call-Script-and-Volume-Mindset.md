# Cold Call Script and Volume Mindset

**Applicable To:** Lead Generator
**Source:** Tyson Smith (Virtual Flip Formula) — Cold Calling Scripts, Volume Mindset, Beginner-to-Deal Path

## The Cold Call Script (2 Sentences)

This script has generated millions in wholesale deals. It is intentionally short:

**Line 1:** "Hey, is this the owner of [ADDRESS]?"

**Line 2:** "Are you interested in selling that property?"

That's it. Nothing more is needed on the opening.

## Why This Script Works

**Address-first throws them off.** Leading with the property address creates curiosity. The seller immediately wonders how you know about their property, which keeps them on the line long enough to hear the question.

**It gets to the point.** There is no small talk, no lengthy introduction, no pitch. The faster you get to the qualifying question, the more calls you can make. More calls equals more deals — the math is unforgiving.

**You are an interviewer, not a salesperson.** The mindset shift is critical. You are not trying to convince anyone of anything. You are simply finding people who are already interested in a cash offer.

> "99% of cold calling is just finding people interested in a cash offer."

## The 3 Fundamentals (Know Before Calling)

Every cold caller must be able to answer these three questions naturally and confidently:

1. **Who you are** — "Me and my partner buy properties for cash in the area"
2. **What you want** — "Looking to buy houses"
3. **Why** — "We rent them out or fix and flip"

These are not scripts to memorize — they are truths to internalize. When a seller asks "Who is this?" or "What do you want?", the answer should flow naturally because you genuinely understand your role.

## Common Objections and Responses

| Objection | Response | Why It Works |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| "How'd you get my number?" | "My partner compiled a list of areas we want to buy in" | Honest, simple, non-threatening |
| "What's your offer?" | **Car Analogy:** "Have you ever test driven a car before buying? Same thing — I need to see the property first. When's good?" | Reframes the conversation from price to appointment |
| "I don't know if I want to sell" | "Before I called, were you even considering it?" If no → move on. If yes → keep going. | Qualifies instantly without pressure |

## The Volume Mindset

Cold calling is a numbers game. The math works if you work the math:

- **Minimum:** 1,000+ dials per day
- **8 hours per day** on the phone
- **2.5 months** average before first deal
- **Fight fire with WATER** — calm beats aggressive every time

> "Every cold call is spinning a roulette board. Most say no. But there are slivers that say $10K, $50K, $100K. If you could spin for free all day, you'd never leave."

The roulette analogy is the most important mental model for cold callers. Every dial is free. Every "no" gets you statistically closer to a "yes." The only way to lose is to stop spinning.

## Energy and Tone

Tone matters more than words. The ideal cold calling voice is:

- **Calm and confident** — not aggressive, not desperate
- **Conversational** — like you're calling a friend about a property
- **Curious** — genuinely interested in whether they want to sell

Script-reading is detectable and kills deals. The goal is to internalize the framework so deeply that it sounds natural every time.

> "Once you taste blood, you're never going back."

The first deal changes everything. The volume mindset that felt impossible suddenly makes perfect sense when you see the wire hit your account.
