# Best Quotes and Core Principles

**Applicable To:** All Roles
**Source:** All 5 Coaches — Motivational Principles, Sales Philosophy, Operational Wisdom

---

## Sales Philosophy

> "We don't sell. We get people to sell themselves."
> — Steve Trang

The highest form of selling is not persuasion — it is facilitation. When you ask the right questions and listen to the answers, the seller talks themselves into the deal. Your job is to guide the conversation, not to push.

> "All sales is tied to emotion, not logic."
> — Todd Toback

Every decision a seller makes is emotional first and logical second. They decide with their gut and justify with their head. Address the emotion, and the logic follows.

> "Frameworks over scripts."
> — Steve Trang

Scripts create robots. Frameworks create professionals. A script tells you what to say. A framework tells you how to think. The difference shows up the moment a conversation goes off-script — which is every conversation.

> "Diagnose before prescribing."
> — Steve Trang

Never present a solution before fully understanding the problem. A doctor who writes a prescription before examining the patient is malpracticing. A wholesaler who makes an offer before understanding the seller's situation is doing the same thing.

> "Professionals sort, amateurs convince."
> — Steve Trang / Todd Toback

Time spent convincing one unmotivated seller is time not spent finding three motivated ones. The math always favors sorting. Qualification is not about being cold — it is about being efficient.

## Rapport and Trust

> "Rapport is greater than price — we were $40K LOWER and still won."
> — Todd Toback

Trust outweighs dollars. A seller who trusts you will accept a lower offer over a higher offer from someone they don't trust. This is not theory — it happens repeatedly in real transactions.

> "How can they object to what they've told you they wanted to buy?"
> — Steve Trang

When you use the seller's own words to present the solution, objections become nearly impossible. They cannot argue against their own stated needs.

## Cold Calling and Volume

> "Every cold call is spinning a roulette board. Most say no. But there are slivers that say $10K, $50K, $100K. If you could spin for free all day, you'd never leave."
> — Tyson Smith

Cold calling is a numbers game with asymmetric upside. Each dial costs nothing but time. The occasional yes is worth thousands. The math is overwhelmingly in your favor if you maintain volume.

> "Every no gets you closer to a yes."
> — Tyson Smith

Rejection is not failure — it is progress. Each no eliminates one non-opportunity and moves you closer to the next opportunity. The only way to lose is to stop dialing.

> "Once you taste blood, you're never going back."
> — Tyson Smith

The first deal changes everything. Before the first deal, cold calling feels like punishment. After the first deal, it feels like opportunity. The mindset shift is permanent.

> "Fight fire with WATER — calm beats aggressive every time."
> — Tyson Smith

Aggression triggers resistance. Calm triggers trust. When a seller is angry, getting calmer — not louder — is the counterintuitive move that works.

## Lead Management and Speed

> "Attack leads like a rabid dog."
> — Todd Toback

Speed to lead is the most impactful metric in the business. The first person to contact a motivated seller wins. Every minute of delay is a competitive disadvantage.

> "Your first $100K month is a systems problem, not a marketing problem. Most businesses have enough leads. They just waste them."
> — Chandler Saine

The bottleneck is rarely lead generation. It is lead management, follow-up discipline, and conversion efficiency. Fix the systems before spending more on marketing.

## Marketing

> "40,000 ads per day. Stand out or die."
> — Courtney Scott

The average person sees 40,000 advertisements per day. In this environment, being average is being invisible. Creative differentiation is not optional — it is survival.

> "Conversion rate is greater than traffic."
> — Courtney Scott

Doubling your conversion rate has the same effect as doubling your traffic — at zero additional cost. Always optimize the funnel before increasing the budget.

---

## Scaling and Financial Discipline

> "Grow profit, not revenue."
> — Chandler Saine

Revenue is vanity. Profit is sanity. A business doing $50K/month with $40K in expenses is less healthy than a business doing $30K/month with $10K in expenses. Focus on what you keep, not what you make.

> "Most wholesalers do $50K months but keep $5K. That's not a business — that's a job with worse hours."
> — Chandler Saine

Without Profit First discipline, revenue growth often means expense growth. The owner works harder, makes more money, and keeps the same amount. This is the scaling trap.

> "4% of activities produce 64% of your outcomes."
> — Chandler Saine

The Pareto principle, compounded. Most of what you do every day does not move the needle. The discipline of identifying and protecting high-value activities is the difference between operators who scale and operators who stall.

> "The biggest mistake is spending on 10 marketing sources."
> — Chandler Saine

Focus beats diversification at every stage of growth. Master one channel before adding another. Spreading budget and attention across unproven channels guarantees mediocre results everywhere.

## Negotiation

> "The worst thing is 'Let's do it!' Because later he thinks 'I could have got more.' Then his sister calls."
> — Todd Toback

Excitement after the close is the enemy of deal retention. The Friction Principle — slowing down after the yes — prevents cancellations and builds lasting commitment.

> "Double your target assignment fee. The size of your deal is what you expect."
> — Todd Toback

Expectations shape outcomes. Wholesalers who expect $5K assignments structure deals for $5K. Wholesalers who expect $20K assignments structure deals for $20K. The market responds to your frame.

> "80% of 'unmotivated' sellers ARE motivated."
> — Todd Toback

Most sellers who initially seem unmotivated are actually motivated but guarded. The qualification process, done with genuine curiosity and without pressure, unlocks motivation that a direct pitch would never reveal.

---

## Application

These quotes and principles are not motivational posters. They are operational guidelines that should influence daily behavior:

- **Call grading** should evaluate whether these principles are being applied in real conversations
- **Coaching sessions** should reference specific principles when providing feedback
- **Training materials** should connect techniques back to the underlying philosophy
- **Team meetings** should reinforce the mindset that drives the methodology

The techniques work because the principles are sound. When the principles are internalized, the techniques become natural.
