# The Complete Wholesale Value Chain

**Applicable To:** All Roles
**Source:** All 5 Coaches — End-to-End Pipeline Overview

---

## The Pipeline

Real estate wholesaling is a pipeline business. Every deal flows through the same stages, and every stage has an owner. Understanding the complete chain — and where your role fits within it — is the foundation of team performance.

```
Lead Generation → First Contact → Qualification → Nurture/Follow-Up → Closing → Disposition → Transaction Close
```

---

## Stage 1: Lead Generation

**Owner:** Marketing Team / Lead Generator
**Primary Coach:** Courtney Scott (Alpha Media)

Leads enter the system through two primary channels:

**Paid acquisition (Meta Ads):** Facebook and Instagram ads drive sellers to a landing page with qualifying questions. Form submissions auto-enter the CRM and trigger immediate automated follow-up (SMS + email). This channel produces higher-quality leads at a higher cost per lead.

**Outbound prospecting (Cold Calling):** Lead generators dial through lists of property owners, using a simple two-line script to identify interest. This channel produces higher volume at lower cost but requires significant labor.

> "Meta lets us engage sellers where they actually spend time."

**Key metric:** Cost per qualified lead. Not cost per lead — cost per lead that actually has motivation, timeline, and equity.

**Handoff to next stage:** A lead is created in the CRM with basic property and contact information.

---

## Stage 2: First Contact

**Owner:** Lead Generator (cold) / Automated System (inbound)
**Primary Coach:** Tyson Smith (Virtual Flip Formula)

For cold calls, first contact happens on the dial itself. The two-line script opens the conversation:

"Hey, is this the owner of [ADDRESS]? Are you interested in selling that property?"

For inbound leads (from ads), first contact is automated: an SMS fires within seconds, and a human call follows within 5 minutes.

> "Speed to lead" is the single most important metric at this stage.

**Key metric:** Speed to lead (time from lead creation to first human contact).

**Handoff to next stage:** If the seller expresses any interest, the lead is passed to the Lead Manager for qualification.

---

## Stage 3: Qualification

**Owner:** Lead Manager
**Primary Coach:** Chandler Saine (Level Up REI)

The Lead Manager's job is to separate motivated sellers from unmotivated ones. This is done through a structured qualification process using five core questions:

1. What's got you thinking about selling?
2. What happens if you don't sell?
3. What's your ideal timeline?
4. Have you spoken with anyone else?
5. If we could work something out that's fair, would you be open to meeting?

> "Professionals sort, amateurs convince."

The Lead Manager does not close deals. They qualify leads and pass only hot, motivated sellers to the Acquisition Manager. This division of labor is the single most impactful structural decision in a wholesaling operation.

**Key metric:** Qualified leads passed to AM per week.

**Handoff to next stage:** Hot leads (motivated + timeline + equity) are passed to the AM with full context. Warm and cold leads enter the follow-up cadence.

---

## Stage 4: Nurture and Follow-Up

**Owner:** Lead Manager (warm/cold leads) / Acquisition Manager (hot leads)
**Primary Coach:** Chandler Saine (Level Up REI) + Courtney Scott (Alpha Media)

Most deals do not close on the first conversation. The follow-up cadence determines how many of those "not yet" leads eventually become deals.

| Lead Temperature | Follow-Up Frequency | Owner |
|-----------------|---------------------|-------|
| Hot (motivated + timeline) | Every 2–3 days | AM |
| Warm (interested, exploring) | Weekly | LM |
| Cold (not now, maybe later) | Bi-weekly to monthly | LM |
| Dead (firm no) | Quarterly automated drip | System |

> "Most businesses have enough leads. They just waste them."

The CRM is the backbone of follow-up. Every lead has a status, a next action date, and an owner. No lead falls through the cracks.

**Key metric:** Follow-up compliance rate (percentage of leads receiving timely follow-up within their cadence).

**Handoff to next stage:** When a warm or cold lead becomes hot (motivation changes, timeline accelerates, circumstances shift), they are re-qualified and passed to the AM.

---

## Stage 5: Closing

**Owner:** Acquisition Manager
**Primary Coaches:** Todd Toback (No Limits REI) + Steve Trang (Disruptors/OPS)

This is where the deal is made. The AM uses Todd's 10-Step Close, Steve's Personality-Adaptive Selling, and Jerry Norton's 7 Seller Personas to navigate the conversation from rapport to signed contract.

The closing process is not a single call. It typically involves:

1. **Discovery call** — Build rapport, uncover motivation, qualify
2. **Property evaluation** — Assess condition, run comps, calculate offer
3. **Offer presentation** — Present the number with context and framing
4. **Objection handling** — Address concerns using the 5 communication techniques
5. **Contract signing** — Execute the purchase agreement
6. **Post-contract retention** — The Friction Principle and Boomerang Close

> "We don't sell. We get people to sell themselves."

**Key metrics:** Offers made, contracts signed, conversion rate (appointment to contract), average assignment fee.

**Handoff to next stage:** A signed purchase agreement is passed to the disposition team.

---

## Stage 6: Disposition

**Owner:** Dispo Manager / Dispo Rep
**Primary Coach:** Jerry Norton (Flipping Mastery)

Disposition is selling the contract to an end buyer. The dispo team packages the deal (photos, comps, repair estimates, profit projections) and distributes it to the buyer's list.

Speed is critical. Every day a deal sits undisposed increases the risk of seller cancellation, title issues, or market shifts.

**Key metrics:** Time to dispo (hours from contract to buyer commitment), assignment fee collected.

**Handoff to next stage:** A buyer is identified and the assignment agreement is executed.

---

## Stage 7: Transaction Close

**Owner:** Transaction Coordinator
**Primary Coach:** Chandler Saine (Level Up REI)

The TC manages the closing process: title work, inspections, document preparation, and coordination between all parties (seller, buyer, title company, lender if applicable).

**Key metric:** Close rate (percentage of contracts that make it to the closing table), days from contract to close.

**Completion:** Wire transfer received. Deal closed. Assignment fee collected.

---

## The Feedback Loop

The value chain is not linear — it is a loop. Every closed deal generates data that improves every stage:

- **Marketing** learns which channels produce deals that actually close (not just leads)
- **Lead generation** learns which seller profiles convert at the highest rate
- **Qualification** refines its criteria based on which leads the AM actually closes
- **Closing** improves through call grading and coaching feedback
- **Disposition** builds buyer relationships that make future deals faster

> "4% of activities produce 64% of your outcomes."

The businesses that scale are the ones that use this feedback loop to continuously optimize each stage, eliminating waste and doubling down on what works.
