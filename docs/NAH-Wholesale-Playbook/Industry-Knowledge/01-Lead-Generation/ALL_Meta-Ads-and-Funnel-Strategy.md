## The Wholesale Lead Generation Funnel

The modern wholesale lead generation funnel follows a predictable path from first impression to closed deal:

```
Meta Ad (FB/IG) → Landing Page (qualifying Qs) → CRM Auto-Entry → Auto Follow-Up (SMS+Email) → Human/AI Call → Appointment → Close
```

This is a full-funnel system — not just "running ads." It encompasses ad creative, landing page optimization, email sequences, retargeting, and CRM integration working together as one machine.

## Key Principles

**Meta over Google PPC.** 94% of people skip PPC ads. Meta (Facebook and Instagram) allows you to engage sellers where they actually spend their time. The targeting capabilities and creative formats make it the superior channel for motivated seller acquisition.

**Conversion rate over traffic.** This is the most important concept in paid lead generation. At $10 cost per click with a 1% conversion rate, each lead costs $1,000. At the same $10 CPC with a 10% conversion rate, each lead costs $100. The math is clear — fix the landing page before increasing ad spend.

**Creative is non-negotiable.** The average person sees 40,000 ads per day. Your ad creative must stand out or it will be invisible. This means testing multiple angles, formats, and hooks constantly. Boring ads produce expensive leads.

**Speed to lead is everything.** Auto-SMS should fire within seconds of a form submission. The first human call should happen within 5 minutes. Every minute of delay reduces conversion probability dramatically.

## Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target Range | Why It Matters |
|--------|-------------|----------------|
| Cost per lead (Meta) | $5–50 | Raw lead acquisition cost |
| Cost per qualified lead | $20–100 | Filters out tire-kickers |
| Cost per contract | $500–3,000 | True cost of a signed deal |
| ROAS (Return on Ad Spend) | 3–10x+ | Overall marketing efficiency |
| Landing page conversion rate | 10%+ | Funnel health indicator |

## Full-Funnel Management

Effective lead generation requires managing every stage, not just the ad itself:

1. **Ad Creative** — Multiple angles tested weekly. Video outperforms static. Authenticity outperforms polish.
2. **Landing Page** — Qualifying questions filter leads before they hit the CRM. Fewer fields means higher conversion but lower quality. Find the balance.
3. **Email Sequences** — Automated nurture sequences keep leads warm between calls.
4. **Retargeting** — Pixel-based retargeting brings back visitors who didn't convert on the first visit.
5. **CRM Integration** — Every lead auto-enters the CRM with source attribution so you can track ROI per channel.

## For Teams Without Ad Budget

If paid advertising isn't feasible, cold calling serves as the free alternative. The same funnel principles apply — you're just replacing the top of the funnel with manual outbound effort. See the Cold Calling materials for the complete approach.

## The ONE Channel Rule

Multiple industry coaches agree on this principle:

> "I see this over and over — wholesalers throwing money at multiple channels with zero focus, zero measurement, zero consistency."

> "The biggest mistake is spending on 10 marketing sources."

The approach: Pick ONE channel. Master it. Scale it. Only then add another. Spreading budget across multiple unproven channels guarantees mediocre results everywhere and mastery nowhere.
