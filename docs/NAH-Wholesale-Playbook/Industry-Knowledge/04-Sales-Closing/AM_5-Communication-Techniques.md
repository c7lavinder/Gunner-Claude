# The 5 Communication Techniques

**Applicable To:** Acquisition Manager
**Source:** Todd Toback (No Limits REI) — Rapport, Negotiation, Communication Mastery

---

## Overview

These five techniques form the communication backbone of every seller conversation. They are not sequential steps — they are tools you deploy situationally throughout a call. A skilled closer weaves all five into a single conversation, choosing the right one based on what the seller just said or did.

> "Rapport is greater than price — we were $40K LOWER and still won."

---

## Technique 1: Cushioning

**What it is:** Softening the conversation before asking a hard question or making a potentially uncomfortable statement. Cushioning removes the adversarial feeling from the interaction.

**When to use it:** Before any question that might make the seller defensive. Before delivering news they might not want to hear. Before transitioning to a new topic.

**Examples:**

"If it's not a fit, that's totally okay. I just want to make sure we explore this properly."

"I don't know if this will work for your situation, but can I ask you a few questions to find out?"

"This might not be the right path for you, and that's fine — but what if we could close in 21 days?"

**Why it works:** Cushioning removes pressure. When a seller feels no pressure, they drop their guard. When their guard is down, they tell you the truth. The truth is where deals live.

**Grading note:** Calls that jump straight to hard questions without cushioning typically score lower on rapport. The seller feels interrogated rather than consulted.

---

## Technique 2: Reversing

**What it is:** Answering a question with a question. Instead of providing information the seller asks for, you redirect to understand why they're asking.

**When to use it:** When the seller asks about your offer price before you've qualified them. When the seller asks a question that, if answered directly, would weaken your position. When you need to understand the motivation behind a question.

**Examples:**

Seller: "What's your offer?"
You: "That's a great question. Before I can give you a number, can I ask — what were you hoping to get?"

Seller: "How fast can you close?"
You: "Speed is definitely something we can work with. Is there a specific timeline you're working against?"

Seller: "Do you buy houses in any condition?"
You: "We do, but help me understand — what's the current condition of the property?"

**Why it works:** The person asking questions controls the conversation. When a seller asks you a question and you answer it directly, they control the frame. When you reverse, you regain control while simultaneously gathering information you need.

**Grading note:** Closers who answer every question directly without reversing tend to give away leverage early. The best calls show strategic reversing in the first 5 minutes.

---

## Technique 3: Truth Telling

**What it is:** Being radically honest with the seller, even when the truth might seem to work against you. Telling the seller something that appears to hurt your position but actually builds trust.

**When to use it:** When the seller has unrealistic expectations. When the seller is comparing your offer to listing with an agent. When you need to establish credibility quickly.

**Examples:**

"Honestly, if getting the absolute highest price is your priority, you'd probably net more listing with a Realtor. It'll take 3–6 months and you'll pay commissions, but you'd likely get more. Is that what you want to do?"

"I'm going to be straight with you — we're not going to be the highest offer. What we offer is speed, certainty, and zero hassle. If that matters to you, we should talk. If not, I don't want to waste your time."

"The truth is, your property needs about $40K in work. That's going to affect what any buyer — us included — can pay. I'd rather be honest about that upfront than surprise you later."

**Why it works:** Truth telling is counterintuitive. By appearing to talk yourself out of the deal, you build massive trust. The seller thinks: "If they're willing to tell me I might do better elsewhere, they must be honest." This trust becomes the foundation for the close.

> "All sales is tied to emotion, not logic."

The emotional impact of honesty outweighs the logical risk of losing the deal. Most sellers, when told they could list with an agent, reveal that they don't want to deal with the hassle — which is exactly the motivation you needed to uncover.

**Grading note:** Truth telling is a high-skill technique. Calls that use it effectively are typically the highest-scoring calls in the system.

---

## Technique 4: Stealth Mismatch

**What it is:** Deliberately presenting the opposite of what the seller expects or wants, causing them to argue themselves back toward your position.

**When to use it:** When the seller is on the fence. When you need the seller to articulate their own motivation. When direct persuasion isn't working.

**Examples:**

Seller: "I think I want to sell."
You: "You know what, it sounds like you're not really ready. Maybe you should hold onto it for another year and see what happens."
Seller: "No, I really do need to sell. The taxes are killing me and I can't keep maintaining it."

Seller: "I'm thinking about your offer."
You: "Honestly, I'm not sure this is the right fit for us either. Let me think about it on my end too."
Seller: "Wait — what do you mean? I thought you wanted to buy it."

**Why it works:** People naturally resist being told what to do. When you push, they pull back. When you pull back, they push forward. Stealth mismatch uses this psychological principle to get the seller to sell themselves on the deal.

This is closely related to Steve Trang's "pull back when they say yes" technique. The underlying principle is the same: create scarcity and remove the feeling of being sold.

**Grading note:** Stealth mismatch is an advanced technique. Its presence in a call usually indicates a skilled closer who reads the conversation well.

---

## Technique 5: Getting Commitments

**What it is:** Defining the next step in painstaking, specific detail before ending any conversation. Never leaving a call without a concrete, time-bound commitment from both parties.

**When to use it:** At the end of every call. After handling an objection. After the seller agrees to anything. Before hanging up.

**Examples:**

"So here's what's going to happen next. I'm going to run the numbers tonight. Tomorrow at 2 PM, I'm going to call you back with a specific offer. If it works for both of us, we'll sign the agreement on Wednesday. Does that work for you?"

"You said you need to talk to your wife. When will that conversation happen? Thursday? Okay. I'll call you Friday at 10 AM. If she's on board, we move forward. If not, no hard feelings. Sound fair?"

"Let me make sure I have this right. You're going to send me the photos by end of day. I'll have the offer to you by Monday morning. If we agree on price, we close in 21 days. Is there anything I'm missing?"

**Why it works:** Vague next steps kill deals. "I'll call you back" means nothing. "I'll call you Tuesday at 3 PM with an offer" means everything. Specific commitments create accountability on both sides and prevent the deal from drifting into limbo.

> "Attack leads like a rabid dog."

The commitment technique ensures that speed to lead and speed through the pipeline are maintained. Every conversation ends with a clear, scheduled next action.

**Grading note:** Calls that end without a specific next step should be flagged for coaching. This is one of the most common and most costly mistakes closers make.

---

## How the 5 Techniques Work Together

In a single call, a skilled closer might:

1. **Cushion** the opening to build comfort
2. **Reverse** the seller's first question to maintain control
3. **Truth tell** about the market to build trust
4. **Stealth mismatch** when the seller hesitates to get them to re-commit
5. **Get a commitment** on the specific next step before hanging up

The techniques are not a sequence. They are a toolkit. The best closers use all five fluidly, choosing the right tool for each moment in the conversation.
