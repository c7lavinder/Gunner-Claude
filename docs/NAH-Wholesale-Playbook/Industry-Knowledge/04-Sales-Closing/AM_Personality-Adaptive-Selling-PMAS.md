# Personality-Adaptive Selling (PMAS)

**Applicable To:** Acquisition Manager
**Source:** Steve Trang (Disruptors/OPS) — Personality-Adaptive Selling, Objection Prevention, Frameworks Over Scripts

---

## Core Philosophy

> "We don't sell. We get people to sell themselves."

> "Frameworks over scripts."

The fundamental principle of personality-adaptive selling is that there is no single "right" way to sell. The right approach depends entirely on who you're talking to. Detecting the seller's personality type in the first 2–3 minutes of conversation and adapting your style accordingly is the highest-leverage skill in sales.

> "How can they object to what they've told you they wanted to buy?"

## The Four Personality Types

Detect which type you're dealing with early. Then adapt everything — your pace, your evidence, your emotional tone, your level of detail.

| Type | How to Identify | How to Sell Them |
|------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Analytical** | Asks detailed questions, wants numbers, skeptical of vague claims | Facts, comps, numbers, proof. Show your work. Let the data speak. |
| **Expressive** | Talks about feelings, shares stories, emotional about the property | Empathy, stories, emotional connection. Mirror their energy. Validate their feelings. |
| **Driver** | Short answers, impatient, wants the bottom line | Get to the point. Be direct. Don't waste their time. Present the offer clearly and quickly. |
| **Amiable** | Agreeable, avoids conflict, needs reassurance | Reassure them. Take your time. No pressure. Make them feel safe. |

## Key Techniques

### Assign a Cost to Inaction

Most sellers underestimate the cost of doing nothing. Make it concrete:

"That property is costing you $X per month in taxes, maintenance, and stress. Over 12 months, that's $Y you're losing by holding onto it."

This reframes the conversation from "how much will you give me" to "how much am I losing by waiting." The seller starts selling themselves on acting now.

### Pull Back When They Say Yes

Counterintuitive but powerful. When a seller shows interest, don't push harder — pull back:

"I'm not sure we're the best fit yet. Let me check a few things on my end."

This creates scarcity and removes the feeling of being "sold." The seller now wants to convince YOU, which is exactly where you want them.

### Use Their Own Words

Listen carefully and reflect their language back to them:

"You said earlier the reason was X. And you just told me Y. So which is it?"

This isn't confrontational — it's clarifying. It forces the seller to reconcile their own statements, which usually reveals the real motivation they've been hiding.

### Diagnose Before Prescribing

> "Diagnose before prescribing."

Never present a solution before fully understanding the problem. A doctor who writes a prescription before examining the patient is malpracticing. A wholesaler who makes an offer before understanding the seller's situation is doing the same thing.

Ask questions. Listen to the answers. Ask follow-up questions. Only when you fully understand the seller's problem, timeline, and constraints should you begin discussing solutions.

## Jerry Norton's 7 Seller Personas

Advanced practitioners use multiple personas within a single call. The goal is to use 3 or more per conversation, switching based on what the seller needs in that moment.

| Persona | When to Use | Key Phrase |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| **Analyst** | Data-driven seller who wants numbers | "Based on the last 3 comps in your area..." |
| **Affirmer** | Emotional or distressed seller | "That must be really hard. I can see why you'd want to get this resolved." |
| **Challenger** | Seller with unrealistic expectations | "Help me understand how that number works with the current market." |
| **Worrier** | Indecisive or procrastinating seller | "What happens if this doesn't sell before the foreclosure date?" |
| **Expert** | Seller confused about their options | "If I were in your shoes, here's what I'd consider..." |
| **Comedian** | Tense or guarded moments | Use humor to humanize yourself and break tension |
| **Idiot** | Seller gives an unreasonably high number | "Help me understand. Maybe I'm missing something here." |

The "Idiot" persona is particularly effective. By playing dumb, you force the seller to justify their position. Most can't — and in the process of trying, they talk themselves down to a more realistic number.

**Grading standard:** Excellent calls use 3 or more personas. Calls stuck in one mode should be flagged for coaching.

## Objection Prevention vs. Objection Handling

The highest level of sales skill is not handling objections well — it's preventing them from arising in the first place.

When you diagnose thoroughly, adapt to personality, use the seller's own words, and build genuine rapport, most objections never surface. The seller has already answered their own concerns through the conversation.

> "Professionals sort, amateurs convince."
