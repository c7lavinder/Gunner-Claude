# Disposition and Deal Distribution

**Applicable To:** Acquisition Manager / Dispo Manager
**Source:** Jerry Norton (Flipping Mastery) — Disposition, Buyer Management, Deal Packaging

---

## What Is Disposition?

Disposition ("dispo") is the process of selling your contracted deal to an end buyer. In wholesaling, you don't buy the property — you sell the contract. Dispo is how you turn a signed purchase agreement into a wire transfer.

## The Buyer's List

The buyer's list is the most valuable asset in a wholesaling business after the team itself. A strong buyer's list means:

- Faster closings (days instead of weeks)
- Higher assignment fees (competitive bidding)
- More deal types you can take (different buyers want different things)

### Building the Buyer's List

| Source | How It Works |
|--------|-------------|
| Cash buyer lists | Public records show recent cash purchases in your market |
| Networking events | REIA meetings, investor meetups, Facebook groups |
| Existing deals | Every buyer who closes becomes a repeat buyer |
| Inbound marketing | "Cash buyers wanted" ads on Facebook/Craigslist |
| Title company referrals | Title companies know who's buying |

### Buyer Segmentation

Not all buyers want the same thing. Segment your list:

| Buyer Type | What They Want | Deal Type |
|-----------|---------------|-----------|
| Fix and flip | Distressed properties with upside | Heavy rehab, cosmetic rehab |
| Buy and hold | Cash-flowing rentals | Turnkey or light rehab in rental areas |
| Landlord | Specific neighborhoods they know | Properties near their existing portfolio |
| Developer | Land or teardowns | Lots, condemned properties, multi-family |
| Novation buyer | Retail-ready properties | Properties that can be listed on MLS |

## Deal Packaging

How you present the deal to buyers matters. A well-packaged deal sells faster and for more money than the same deal presented poorly.

### What to Include in a Deal Package

1. **Property photos** — Exterior and interior. Even phone photos are better than nothing.
2. **Property details** — Address, beds, baths, sqft, lot size, year built
3. **ARV (After Repair Value)** — Supported by 3–5 comparable sales
4. **Repair estimate** — Itemized if possible, ballpark if not
5. **Contract price** — What you have it under contract for
6. **Assignment fee** — What you're asking
7. **Buyer's all-in cost** — Contract price + assignment fee + estimated repairs
8. **Potential profit** — ARV minus buyer's all-in cost

### The Math Buyers Care About

Buyers evaluate deals with one simple formula:

```
Profit = ARV - (Purchase Price + Assignment Fee + Repairs + Holding Costs + Selling Costs)
```

If the profit is attractive enough for the buyer's risk tolerance, they'll move. If it's not, no amount of salesmanship will make the deal work.

**Rule of thumb for fix-and-flip buyers:** They want to be all-in at 70% of ARV or less. So:

```
Max Offer = (ARV × 0.70) - Repairs - Assignment Fee
```

## Speed of Disposition

The faster you dispo a deal, the less risk you carry. Best practices:

- **Blast the deal within 24 hours** of signing the contract
- **Follow up with interested buyers within 1 hour** of their response
- **Schedule property access within 48 hours** for serious buyers
- **Have a backup buyer** before accepting the first offer

> "A deal sitting on your desk is a deal at risk."

Every day a deal sits undisposed increases the risk of the seller backing out, title issues surfacing, or market conditions shifting.

## Common Dispo Mistakes

1. **Overpricing the assignment fee** — Greed kills deals. A $10K assignment fee that closes is worth more than a $25K fee that falls apart.
2. **Not having enough buyers** — If you're relying on 2–3 buyers, you're one "no" away from a dead deal.
3. **Poor deal packaging** — Buyers who can't quickly evaluate the deal will skip it and look at the next one.
4. **Slow follow-up with buyers** — Active investors review dozens of deals per week. If you're slow, they've moved on.
5. **Not building relationships** — The best buyers are repeat buyers. Treat them well, deliver honest deal packages, and they'll buy from you again and again.
