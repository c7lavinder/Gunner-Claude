# The Lead Manager System and Qualification

**Applicable To:** Lead Manager
**Source:** Chandler Saine (Level Up REI) — KPIs, Scaling, Team Building, Lead Manager System

---

## The Lead Manager Role

The Lead Manager is the single most impactful hire in a wholesaling operation. Before the Acquisition Manager ever touches a lead, the LM has already done the critical work of qualifying, nurturing, and filtering.

> "Three times in 30 days, we hired a lead manager on the FIRST call. They literally 2x'd their business."

## What the Lead Manager Does

The LM is the gatekeeper between raw leads and the closer. Their responsibilities are:

1. **Calls ALL new leads** — whether outbound (cold list) or inbound (from marketing)
2. **Qualifies each lead** — Is the seller motivated? What's the timeline? Is there equity? What's the property condition?
3. **Passes ONLY hot leads to the AM** — The AM's time is the most expensive resource in the company. Protecting it is the LM's primary job.
4. **Manages the CRM** — No lead falls through the cracks. Every contact is logged, every follow-up is scheduled, every status is updated.

## What the Lead Manager Needs

Three things make an LM effective:

1. **A sales script** — Not to read verbatim, but to internalize as a framework for every conversation
2. **A process to follow** — Clear rules for when to escalate, when to nurture, when to disqualify
3. **A dialed-in CRM** — The system must be configured so the LM can work efficiently without manual tracking

## Follow-Up Cadence

Follow-up is where most wholesaling businesses fail. Leads are expensive to generate and cheap to nurture. The cadence should be:

| Lead Temperature | Follow-Up Frequency | Who Owns It |
|-----------------|--------------------|-----------| 
| Hot (motivated + timeline) | Every 2–3 days | Acquisition Manager |
| Warm (interested, exploring) | Weekly | Lead Manager |
| Cold (not now, maybe later) | Bi-weekly to monthly | Lead Manager |
| Dead (firm no) | Quarterly drip | Automated |

The key insight: "dead" leads are not truly dead. Circumstances change. A seller who said "no" six months ago may have just received a tax lien notice. Quarterly automated drips keep the door open at near-zero cost.

## Qualification Framework

The LM must determine four things on every call:

1. **Motivation** — Does the seller have a real reason to sell? (Divorce, foreclosure, inheritance, relocation, tired landlord, tax burden)
2. **Timeline** — When do they need this resolved? Urgency separates hot leads from warm ones.
3. **Equity** — Is there enough spread between what they owe and what the property is worth to make a deal work?
4. **Condition** — What state is the property in? This affects ARV calculations and buyer interest.

A lead that scores high on all four is hot and goes to the AM immediately. A lead that scores high on motivation but low on timeline goes into the warm nurture cadence.

## The Disqualification Skill

Knowing when to disqualify is as important as knowing when to escalate. Signs a lead should be disqualified:

- No motivation and no foreseeable change in circumstances
- Owes more than the property is worth (no equity)
- Unrealistic price expectations with no flexibility
- Property in an unmarketable location or condition

> "Professionals sort, amateurs convince."

The LM's job is to sort leads efficiently, not to convince unmotivated sellers to sell. Time spent convincing one unmotivated seller is time not spent finding three motivated ones.

## KPIs for Lead Managers

| Metric | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------------|
| Speed to lead | Time from lead entry to first contact |
| Calls per day | Volume of outbound activity |
| Conversations per day | Actual two-way conversations (not voicemails) |
| Appointments set | Qualified leads passed to AM |
| Follow-up rate | Percentage of leads with scheduled next contact |
| Conversion rate | Leads → Appointments → Contracts |

> "Your first $100K month is a systems problem, not a marketing problem. Most businesses have enough leads. They just waste them."
