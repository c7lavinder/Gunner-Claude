# Follow-Up Cadence and CRM Discipline

**Applicable To:** Lead Manager
**Source:** Chandler Saine (Level Up REI) — KPIs, Scaling, Lead Manager System

---

## Why Follow-Up Is the Highest-ROI Activity

Most wholesaling businesses generate enough leads. They just waste them. The difference between a $30K month and a $100K month is almost never more leads — it's better follow-up on the leads you already have.

> "Your first $100K month is a systems problem, not a marketing problem."

## The Daily Optimizer (Time Audit)

Before optimizing follow-up, optimize how you spend your time:

1. List every task that takes your attention during a workday
2. Grade each task 0–3:
   - **0** = No revenue impact
   - **1** = Minimal revenue impact
   - **2** = Moderate revenue impact
   - **3** = Highest revenue impact
3. Tasks graded 0 and 1 → delegate or automate immediately
4. Tasks graded 2 and 3 → do MORE of these

> "4% of activities produce 64% of your outcomes."

For Lead Managers, the highest-value activities are almost always: making calls, having conversations, and setting appointments. Everything else (data entry, list management, reporting) should be minimized or automated.

## CRM Discipline Rules

The CRM is the Lead Manager's primary tool. It must be maintained with absolute discipline:

1. **Every contact is logged** — No call happens without a CRM note. If it's not in the CRM, it didn't happen.
2. **Every lead has a next action** — No lead should exist in the CRM without a scheduled follow-up date. "Orphan" leads with no next action are money left on the table.
3. **Status is always current** — Lead temperature (hot/warm/cold/dead) must reflect reality, not last week's reality.
4. **Pipeline stages are accurate** — If a lead moved from "Interested" to "Appointment Set," the CRM reflects it the same day.
5. **Notes are useful** — "Called, no answer" is not a useful note. "Called, no answer, 3rd attempt, try again Thursday morning — seller works nights" is useful.

## Orphan Lead Scanning

An orphan lead is any contact in the CRM with no scheduled next action. These represent the single biggest source of lost revenue in most wholesaling operations.

A weekly orphan scan should identify:
- Leads with no follow-up scheduled
- Leads whose last contact was more than 2 weeks ago (for warm leads) or 1 month ago (for cold leads)
- Leads that were marked "callback" but never called back

Every orphan lead should be either re-engaged, re-scheduled, or formally disqualified. There is no acceptable middle ground.

## Speed to Lead

The data on speed to lead is unambiguous:

- Leads contacted within **5 minutes** convert at dramatically higher rates than leads contacted after 30 minutes
- After **1 hour**, the probability of qualifying a lead drops by over 80%
- After **24 hours**, the lead is effectively cold regardless of how they came in

For inbound leads (from marketing), the system should auto-fire an SMS within seconds and queue a human call within 5 minutes. For outbound leads (cold lists), the cadence is less urgent but consistency matters more.
